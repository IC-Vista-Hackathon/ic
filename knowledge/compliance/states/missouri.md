# Missouri — Payments Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Source (Credit Card vs. ACH)
- No Missouri-specific statute identified restricting recurring/autopay funding source to ACH/bank debit (no FL-style utility/insurance carve-out found).
- Preauthorized EFT/ACH debits are governed by federal Reg E (12 CFR 205.10) and the federal EFTA (15 U.S.C. § 1693 et seq.); Missouri credit unions/associations reference these directly (20 CSR 1140-25.010).
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** if a Missouri-specific carve-out exists for specific verticals (utilities, insurance).

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No general state-law prohibition on merchant credit card surcharges; Missouri takes a hands-off approach (subject to federal card-network rules, e.g., Visa 3% cap, federal 4% cap).
- **Mo. Rev. Stat. § 408.140** — governs additional charges/fees; convenience-fee disclosure must state the dollar amount of the fee, whether it's nonrefundable, and that it is charged for use of a specific payment method.
- 2024 attempt to tighten surcharge/convenience-fee rules (HB 2383) did not pass.
- SB 354 (prior session) proposed prohibiting merchant credit/debit surcharge fees — did not become law; surcharging remains permitted.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option
- **Mo. Rev. Stat. § 407.675** (Merchandising Practices Act, Ch. 407) — contracts (as enacted, scoped to buyers' clubs/discount buying organizations) may not exceed 24 months and may **not contain an automatic renewal clause** (renewal must be consumer-initiated); duration must be disclosed in boldface, min. 14-point type.
- No broad, general-purpose automatic-renewal/negative-option statute covering all consumer subscriptions was identified (§ 407.675 is narrow in scope). Recent bills (HB 1029, HB 1246, HB 3512, HB 2163) to broaden auto-renewal notice requirements have been introduced across sessions — **none confirmed enacted**; check current session status before relying on this being unregulated broadly.
- General Merchandising Practices Act (Ch. 407) unfair/deceptive practices provisions would apply to negative-option/recurring billing practices generally.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **Mo. Rev. Stat. § 407.1500** (enacted 2009).
- "Personal information" expressly includes: name + financial account number, credit card number, or debit card number, in combination with any required security code/access code/password.
- Notice to affected consumers: "without unreasonable delay."
- AG notification required when notice given to **more than 1,000** MO consumers at one time; nationwide CRAs must also be notified.
- Enforcement: AG has exclusive authority; civil penalty up to **$150,000** per breach/series of related breaches.

## 5. PCI-DSS Reference in Statute
- No Missouri statute identified that references or mandates PCI-DSS compliance (unlike Nevada NRS 603A.215). PCI-DSS compliance in Missouri is contractual (card brand/acquirer agreements) only.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** if any newer session has introduced a PCI-referencing bill.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes
- No standalone Missouri EFT/ACH consumer-protection statute beyond adoption/reference to federal EFTA (15 U.S.C. § 1693) and Reg E (12 CFR 205), as reflected in 20 CSR 1140-25.010 (credit union remote service units).
