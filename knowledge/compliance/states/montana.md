# Montana — Payments Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Source (Credit Card vs. ACH)
- No Montana-specific statute identified restricting recurring/autopay funding source to ACH/bank debit for any vertical (utility, insurance, etc.).
- Preauthorized EFTs generally governed by federal EFTA/Reg E; Montana's own EFT Act (Title 32, Ch. 6, MCA) addresses definitions/regulation of EFT systems, not a CC-vs-ACH funding restriction.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- **Mont. Code Ann. § 7-6-617** (local government fee/tax payment by credit card) and **§ 61-3-117** (state agency fee payment by credit card) — permit surcharge/convenience fees on government payments.
- General merchant surcharging: permitted, capped at **3%** of the transaction (lower than the federal/card-network 4% ceiling); surcharge must be clearly disclosed before the transaction completes; may not be applied to debit cards.
- Notable consumer protection: Montana law bars financial institutions/card networks from **prohibiting** merchants from imposing the surcharge (i.e., protects the merchant's right to surcharge).

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option
- **Montana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Act ("MUTPCPA")** — Mont. Code Ann. §§ 30-14-101 to 30-14-142 (Title 30, Ch. 14, Part 1). Broadly prohibits "unfair methods of competition and unfair or deceptive acts or practices" in trade/commerce (§ 30-14-103).
- Remedies: private right of action, up to **treble damages**, equitable relief; AG/Dept. of Justice enforcement (Office of Consumer Protection).
- Recently amended (SB 488, effective 2025) to explicitly cover false/misleading/deceptive consumer reviews and testimonials.
- Auto-renewal: a Montana automatic-renewal provision applies specifically to natural gas/utility-type service contracts (transmission, energy, metered, billing services) — **confirm exact citation before relying**; no evidence found of a broad, California-style general auto-renewal statute covering all consumer subscriptions.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **Mont. Code Ann. §§ 30-14-1704 to 30-14-1706** (general breach notification, Dept. of Justice/Office of Consumer Protection).
- A parallel insurance-sector breach provision exists at **Mont. Code Ann. § 33-19-321** (Title 33 — Insurance) — relevant if InvoiceCloud processes payments on behalf of MT-licensed insurance billers.
- "Personal information" includes: name + account number or credit/debit card number, in combination with any required security code/access code/password permitting access to a financial account.
- Notice: "without unreasonable delay," consistent with law enforcement needs/scope investigation.
- Businesses must simultaneously submit an electronic copy of the notice to the Office of Consumer Protection (no numeric consumer-count threshold identified for AG copy — differs from many states' 500/1,000-consumer trigger; **confirm current threshold with counsel**).

## 5. PCI-DSS Reference in Statute
- No Montana statute identified that mandates or references PCI-DSS compliance. Montana state entities (university system, DOT) apply PCI-DSS only via processor/card-brand contractual requirements, not state law.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes
- **Montana Electronic Funds Transfer Act — Title 32, Chapter 6, MCA** (definitions/regulatory framework for EFT systems in the state, largely tracking federal EFTA/Reg E).
- No identified statute imposing additional consumer-protection restrictions on recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E § 1005.10.
