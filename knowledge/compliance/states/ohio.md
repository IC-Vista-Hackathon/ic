# Ohio — Payments Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.*

## 1. Autopay / Recurring Payment Funding Source Restrictions
- No Ohio statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only vs. credit card generally.
- CRES (competitive retail electric service) auto-renewal contracts (OAC 4901:1-21-11/-12, 4901:1-29-10) impose notice requirements but no funding-method restriction identified.
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel on Ohio-specific insurance premium autopay rules; no statute located restricting insurance/utility autopay to ACH-only comparable to FL's rule.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- **Currently no enacted Ohio statute** prohibits or specifically regulates credit card surcharging; convenience fees are presently legal regardless of card type. Merchants are bound by federal law and card-network rules only.
- **Pending bill**: Senate Bill 337 (136th General Assembly) — "require disclosure of credit card surcharges" — would create new R.C. § 1345.29 requiring clear/conspicuous point-of-sale disclosure of surcharges; violation would be an unfair/deceptive act under the Consumer Sales Practices Act (CSPA), AG-enforceable. **Status: introduced/referred to Senate Financial Institutions, Insurance & Technology Committee — NOT yet enacted.** Confirm current status before relying on it.
- R.C. § 113.40 governs acceptance of financial transaction devices for state expense payments (state government context, not general private-merchant law).
- R.C. § 503.55 addresses township acceptance of payments via financial transaction devices (local government context).

## 3. UDAP / Consumer Protection — Recurring Billing, Negative Option, Autorenewal
- **R.C. § 1345.02** — Ohio Consumer Sales Practices Act (CSPA): bars unfair or deceptive acts/practices by a "supplier" in connection with a consumer transaction, before/during/after the transaction; lists specific deceptive practices (false sponsorship/approval claims, false "new" claims, operating without required license/bond/insurance, etc.). Courts must give "due consideration and great weight" to FTC Act interpretations/orders.
- **R.C. § 1345.03** — bars unconscionable consumer sales acts/practices (separate CSPA provision, relevant to unfair recurring-billing or negative-option terms).
- No comprehensive Ohio statute specifically targets automatic renewal/negative-option billing at the general consumer level (Ohio was noted, as of 2019, among states without such comprehensive legislation). Sector-specific auto-renewal notice rules exist for CRES electric contracts (OAC 4901:1-21-11, 4901:1-21-12, 4901:1-29-10) — confirm current text and applicability if in scope.
- Federal ROSCA applies by default in absence of state-specific negative-option statute.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **R.C. § 1349.19** — "Private disclosure of security breach of computerized personal information data":
  - Requires disclosure to affected Ohio residents when a breach causes or is reasonably believed to cause a material risk of identity theft/fraud.
  - Disclosure required "in the most expedient time possible" and no later than 45 days after discovery.
  - "Personal information" = name (or initial) + SSN, driver's license/state ID number, or **account/credit card number combined with a security code, access code, or password permitting access to the account** — directly covers payment card data.
  - Financial institutions/trust companies/credit unions (and affiliates) already subject to federal breach-notice requirements are exempt from this section.
  - AG enforcement: fines up to $1,000/day (first 60 days), up to $5,000/day (days 61+), up to $10,000/day (after 90 days) of non-compliance.

## 5. PCI-DSS References in State Law
- **R.C. Chapter 1354** — "Ohio Data Protection Act" (2018, SB 220): voluntary cybersecurity safe-harbor (affirmative defense to certain tort claims arising from a data breach) for entities that create/maintain/comply with a written cybersecurity program reasonably conforming to a recognized framework.
- **R.C. § 1354.02** lists qualifying frameworks: NIST CSF, ISO/IEC 27000, CIS Controls, FedRAMP, and PCI-DSS — **but per counsel commentary, PCI-DSS alone is treated as insufficient standalone coverage; entities relying on PCI-DSS should pair it with another listed framework when cardholder data is in scope.** Sector frameworks (HIPAA/HITECH, GLBA, FISMA) also qualify for covered entities.
- This is a safe harbor (defense), not a compliance mandate — participation is voluntary.

## 6. State EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes (beyond federal Reg E)
- No Ohio-specific consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E was identified in this research pass.
- R.C. § 301.28 and the Ohio Treasurer's EFT program (eft.tos.ohio.gov) govern state tax/payment ACH credit/debit remittance — government payment context, not general consumer EFT law.
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel for any Ohio Division of Financial Institutions rule bearing on consumer EFT beyond Reg E.
