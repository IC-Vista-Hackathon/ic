# North Dakota — Payments Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.*

## 1. Autopay / Recurring Payment Funding Source Restrictions
- No ND statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only vs. credit card.
- Automatic renewal statute (N.D.C.C. ch. 51-37, below) is funding-method agnostic.
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel for any ND Public Service Commission utility-specific rule.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No ND statute prohibits or caps credit card surcharging; state defers to federal law and card network rules (practical ceiling ~3% per Visa's 2023 cap).
- Card networks require 30-day advance notice from merchants electing to surcharge (network rule, not ND statute).
- N.D.C.C. Title 12.5, ch. 12.5-03-01 ("State Credit Card Administration") governs state government merchant card administration only — not private merchant surcharging.
- Debit/prepaid card surcharging remains barred under federal card-network rules nationwide.

## 3. UDAP / Consumer Protection — Recurring Billing, Negative Option, Autorenewal
- **N.D.C.C. Chapter 51-15** — "Unlawful Sales or Advertising Practices" (ND Consumer Fraud Act): bars deceptive acts/practices, fraud, false pretense/promise, misrepresentation in connection with sale/advertisement of merchandise (defined broadly to include goods, services, real estate) — applies regardless of actual reliance/damage. Also bars unconscionable acts causing substantial, unavoidable consumer injury. AG-enforced; private plaintiffs may recover actual damages, treble damages if "knowing" violation, plus attorney's fees.
- **N.D.C.C. Chapter 51-37** — "Customer Contract Clauses and Automatic Renewals" (enacted 2019 via HB 1195, effective for contracts after 7/31/2019):
  - Applies to auto-renewal terms exceeding 6 months.
  - Requires clear and conspicuous written notice of the right to cancel and avoid renewal, including the cancellation procedure.
  - Notice must be given 30–60 days before the renewal/cancellation-deadline date.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **N.D.C.C. Chapter 51-30** — "Notice of Security Breach for Personal Information" (enacted 2005; amended by HB 1435 (2013), SB 2214 (2015)):
  - Breach = unauthorized acquisition of computerized data where personal information was not secured by encryption or equivalent.
  - "Personal information" = name + covered data element (incl. financial account/card number combined with security code/access code/password where required for account access — confirm current definition text for payment-card-specific language).
  - Notice required "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay."
  - AG notification required if breach affects 250+ individuals.
  - AG enforcement only; civil penalty up to $5,000 per violation. No private right of action.
- **HB 1127 (signed 2025)** — establishes new data security measures and breach notification obligations specifically for financial corporations; may be relevant to payments entities — not confirmed in detail, verify current codified location and applicability with counsel.

## 5. PCI-DSS References in State Law
- No ND statute directly mandates or references PCI-DSS. Enforced solely via merchant agreements/card network contracts, not state law.

## 6. State EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes (beyond federal Reg E)
- **N.D.C.C. Title 6, Chapter 6-08.1** — "Disclosure of Customer Information": governs bank customer-information disclosure/consent, not EFT mechanics directly.
- **N.D.C.C. § 6-08-16.1 / 6-08-16.2** — criminal penalties for authorizing an EFT against an account at an institution where the person holds no account (fraud-type provision), not a general consumer EFT framework.
- No broader ND consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E was identified. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
