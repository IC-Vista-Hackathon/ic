# Georgia — Payment Compliance Reference

*Internal reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Method (Credit Card vs ACH)

- **No Georgia statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH/bank debit over credit card.** No general or insurance-specific (O.C.G.A. Title 33) provision located that bars credit-card-funded recurring billing.
- O.C.G.A. § 33-24-59.24 (Title 33, Ch. 24 — Insurance Generally) — "Restrictions on Payment Methods Prohibited" — runs the **other direction**: it prohibits health plans from restricting provider payment (insurer → provider) to credit-card-only methods. Not a consumer autopay funding restriction; noted for completeness since it's the closest "payment method restriction" hit in Title 33.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel:** whether any Georgia Department of Insurance rule/bulletin or premium finance statute (O.C.G.A. Title 33, Ch. 22 — Premium Finance Companies) imposes a funding-method restriction.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law

- O.C.G.A. § 10-1-393.3 and related Fair Business Practices Act provisions: surcharges may not exceed the merchant's actual cost of accepting the card (no profit on surcharge).
- Convenience fees on card transactions are permitted only if the merchant also accepts an alternative (non-card) payment method.
- Nondisclosure of surcharge/fee amounts can independently violate FBPA deceptive-advertising provisions.
- Enforcement: Georgia AG Consumer Protection Division under the Fair Business Practices Act (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.); penalties $200–$1,000 per violation under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-421 (per secondary source — confirm exact citation with counsel).
- Federal card-network rules independently cap surcharges (commonly cited at ≤4%, network-rule-driven, not a Georgia statutory cap).

## 3. UDAP / Recurring Billing / Auto-Renewal

- **Fair Business Practices Act (FBPA)** — O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.: prohibits unfair/deceptive acts or practices in consumer transactions.
- **Automatic Renewal / Service Contracts** — O.C.G.A. Title 13, Ch. 12 (§§ 13-12-1 through 13-12-5), effective 2013:
  - Applies to auto-renewal provisions extending a service contract >1 month where total term exceeds 6 months.
  - Auto-renewal term must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously.
  - For contracts ≥12 months with renewal periods >1 month: written/electronic notice required 30–60 days before the cancellation deadline.
  - **Enhanced consent for long-term auto-renewals** (renewal period >24 months): seller must obtain the consumer's written/electronic acknowledgment of the notice **and** an affirmative response that the consumer does not intend to cancel — stronger than Florida's equivalent provision.
- **Negative option plans**: no dedicated Georgia statute located; state has historically relied on incorporation of the FTC's Negative Option Rule (16 C.F.R. § 425) by reference (per Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 122-4, per secondary source — confirm with counsel).

## 4. Data Breach Notification (Payment Card Data)

- O.C.G.A. § 10-1-912 (part of FBPA framework, Title 10 Ch. 1 Art. 34).
- Applies to "information brokers"/"data collectors" holding computerized personal information of Georgia residents, including financial/credit/debit account numbers usable without additional identifiers.
- Notice: "most expedient time possible... without unreasonable delay."
- Third-party processors/vendors holding data on behalf of a data collector must notify that data collector within **24 hours** of discovering a breach.
- Breaches affecting >10,000 GA residents also require notice to nationwide consumer reporting agencies.
- Encryption/redaction safe harbor applies.
- No private right of action; no dedicated regulatory penalty scheme under this section (enforced via FBPA generally, per secondary sources).

## 5. PCI-DSS Statutory References

- No Georgia statute found that expressly codifies or references PCI-DSS as a legal requirement. It functions purely as a card-network contractual standard in Georgia, same as federally.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes (Beyond Reg E)

- No Georgia-specific consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E/EFTA identified in this research pass.
