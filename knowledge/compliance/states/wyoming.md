# Wyoming — Payment Compliance Reference

Not legal advice. Internal compliance reference only — verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment — Credit Card vs ACH Restrictions
- No Wyoming statute found restricting funding source (credit card vs ACH/bank debit) for recurring/autopay billing. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Wyoming's notable payment-related quirk is on the *discount* side, not autopay funding: Wyo. Stat. § 40-14-209 caps cash/check discounts (non-credit-card payment inducements) at 5%, and requires the discount be offered to all prospective buyers and disclosed clearly per the federal Consumer Credit Protection Act. This is a discount cap, not a recurring-payment funding restriction.
- No FL-style ACH-only mandate for utility/insurance autopay identified.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Laws
- No Wyoming statute directly regulating credit card surcharging — surcharging is permitted, subject to federal rules (effective cap ~4%; card network rules, e.g., Visa, cap at 3%). Debit card surcharging prohibited (national rule).
- Distinctive Wyoming rule: **Wyo. Stat. § 40-14-209** — cash/check/non-credit-card discounts capped at 5%; must be offered to all buyers; disclosure required per federal CCPA. Wyoming is described as the only state capping cash discounts this way.
- 2012 HB0064 and 2026 HB0097 reference surcharge-adjacent legislative activity — **status/effect not confirmed, verify with counsel**.

## 3. UDAP / Consumer Protection — Recurring Billing, Negative Option, Autorenewal
- **Wyoming Consumer Protection Act, Wyo. Stat. § 40-12-101 et seq.** (enacted 1973, amended 2014).
  - § 40-12-105 — "Unlawful practices": prohibits deceptive trade practices in consumer transactions, including false representations of source/origin/sponsorship, false standards/grades, false claims that repair/replacement is needed, and general "unfair or deceptive acts or practices."
  - § 40-12-104 — related unlawful-practices provision; AG or private party may bring action for injunction (public interest) or actual damages (private, after failure to cure).
- **No dedicated Wyoming automatic-renewal/negative-option statute identified.** Wyoming is among states without a specific auto-renewal law; general UDAP language under § 40-12-105 is the only apparent recourse for autorenewal-related complaints. **Verify current legislative status with counsel** — auto-renewal bills have been introduced in various states recently and Wyoming's position could change.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **Wyo. Stat. § 40-12-501 (Definitions) and § 40-12-502 (Computer Security Breach; Notice to Affected Persons)**, within Title 40, Ch. 12, Art. 5.
- Applies to any individual/commercial entity conducting business in Wyoming that owns/licenses computerized personal identifying information of Wyoming residents (including out-of-state entities).
- Covered data expressly includes "financial account number, credit card number, or debit card number combined with any security code, access code, or password providing financial account access."
- Requires reasonable/prompt investigation upon discovery; if misuse occurred or is reasonably likely, notice must be given "as soon as possible and without unreasonable delay."
- Enforced by Wyoming Attorney General.
- Note: Article 5 also covers credit freeze/report provisions (§§ 40-12-501–509) — adjacent but distinct from breach notice duty.

## 5. PCI-DSS References in Statute
- No Wyoming statute found that names or incorporates PCI-DSS as a compliance requirement.
- Secondary commentary (PivIT Strategy) notes PCI-DSS applies as an industry/card-network requirement to WY businesses processing card data, but this is not a statutory mandate. **Not confirmed as statutory — verify with counsel.**

## 6. EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes Beyond Federal Reg E
- No dedicated Wyoming consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E identified.
- Wyo. Stat. Title 13, Ch. 1, Art. 5 (§ 13-1-501 et seq.) — "Remote Electronic Banking Facilities," defines remote electronic terminals (ATMs, automated loan machines, etc.); excludes POS terminals, telephones, and consumer-operated PCs. This is a banking-facility/definitions statute, not a general consumer EFT law.
- Wyoming Money Transmitters Act (Title 40, Ch. 22) governs money transmission generally, with an exemption for electronic transfer of government benefits per Reg E — not consumer-recurring-payment specific.
