# Maine Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Maine statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only (vs. credit card) for utilities, insurance, or generally. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Note interaction effect: because Maine bans card surcharging outright (§2), a merchant cannot recoup card-processing cost via a card-specific autopay surcharge/fee — but this is a surcharge ban, not a funding-method mandate.
- Insurance premiums specifically: under the Insurance Code, producers may not add fees to premiums (only insurers set premium rates) — relevant if considering insurance-premium autopay via card.
- Governed by federal EFTA/Reg E for EFT/ACH-funded recurring payments.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- Maine is one of the few states with an outright ban on card surcharging: 9-A M.R.S. § 8-509 ("Credit card and debit card surcharge prohibition") prohibits sellers from increasing the regular price to a cardholder in a manner not imposed on cash/check payers.
- Cash discounts remain permitted if the discount is prominently posted for all customers.
- Governmental-entity exception: a government entity may surcharge for card payment of taxes, fines, charges, utility fees, regulatory/registration/license/permit fees, or specific goods/services, if disclosed clearly before payment and the surcharge does not exceed the entity's actual card-processing cost.
- Debit card surcharging additionally banned nationwide under the federal Durbin Amendment.
- Recent repeal attempts (e.g., LD 660/SP 222, 128th Legislature) to allow surcharges have been introduced — confirm current status/session outcome with counsel before relying on the ban long-term.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act — 5 M.R.S. § 207: declares unfair methods of competition and unfair/deceptive acts or practices in trade/commerce unlawful; courts guided by FTC Act §5 interpretations. AG rulemaking authority; civil penalty up to $10,000 per intentional violation (§209 area); private remedies under § 213 (actual damages, restitution, injunction) for personal/family/household purchases.
- Automatic Subscription/Health Club Renewal law — 10 M.R.S. Chapter 205-B (§§ 1210-C, 1210-D):
  - Applies to agreements entered/renewed on or after January 1, 2020.
  - Requires express consumer consent to auto-renewal before enrollment (checkbox, e-signature, or other affirmative act).
  - Requires cancellation available by the same method/manner used to enroll.
  - Toll-free, prominently displayed cancellation phone number if a phone option is offered; disclosure must cover charge amount/frequency and refund policy.
  - 2025 amendment (SP 650) adds a requirement for separate consumer consent specifically to the auto-renewal provision, applicable to agreements entered/renewed on or after January 1, 2026 — confirm final enacted section numbering with counsel.
  - § 1210-D covers violations/enforcement.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Notice of Risk to Personal Data Act — 10 M.R.S. Chapter 210-B, § 1348 (Security breach notice requirements); definitions at § 1347; enforcement/penalties at § 1349.
- Requires reasonable/prompt investigation and notice to affected Maine residents when computerized personal information (including payment card data — confirm exact definitional scope at § 1347) is/may have been acquired by an unauthorized person.
- Notice generally required within 30 days absent law-enforcement delay.
- Notice to nationwide consumer reporting agencies required without unreasonable delay if breach affects more than 1,000 persons.
- Notice to state regulator (Dept. of Professional and Financial Regulation, or AG if unregulated) also required.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Maine statute found that independently mandates PCI-DSS compliance by law. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- PCI-DSS compliance in Maine is a card-network/processor contractual obligation, not a state statutory mandate; Maine's principal statutory security lever is the breach-notification law (§4).

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Maine consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for private recurring payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
