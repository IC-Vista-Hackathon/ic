# Hawaii — Payment Compliance Reference

*Internal reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Method (Credit Card vs ACH)

- **No Hawaii statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH/bank debit over credit card**, for insurance or otherwise. Nothing identified in HRS Title 24 (Insurance) or general commerce statutes.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** if a targeted secondary search is needed against HRS Ch. 431 (Insurance Code) premium-finance provisions.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law

- **Hawaii has no operative statute prohibiting credit card surcharges.** HRS § 481B-14 does not exist as an enacted surcharge ban — bills (SB 470, SB 2745, 2013–2014 sessions) proposing a new "Credit Card Surcharges Prohibited" section under HRS Ch. 481B were introduced but never passed.
- Surcharging in Hawaii is therefore governed only by federal law and card-network rules (cap/disclosure), not a state surcharge statute.
- HRS § 478-11.5 addresses credit cards in a different, narrower context (confirm scope with counsel if relevant to a specific product).

## 3. UDAP / Recurring Billing / Auto-Renewal

- **HRS § 480-2** (Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices, "little FTC Act"): declares unfair methods of competition and unfair/deceptive acts or practices unlawful; construed with deference to FTC/federal court interpretations of FTC Act §5(a)(1). Enforceable by the Hawaii Attorney General, Office of Consumer Protection, or a private consumer plaintiff.
- **HRS § 481-9.5** (Automatic renewal clauses and continuous service clauses): a person who knowingly violates automatic-renewal/continuous-service contract provisions, or knowingly fails to effect a requested cancellation, is deemed to have committed an unfair or deceptive act or practice under § 480-2 — i.e., auto-renewal violations are enforced through the UDAP statute rather than a standalone penalty scheme.

## 4. Data Breach Notification (Payment Card Data)

- HRS Ch. 487N, § 487N-2 (Security Breach of Personal Information).
- Applies to any business (or government agency) that owns/licenses/maintains personal information of Hawaii residents — notably **covers paper records as well as computerized data**, broader than many states.
- Notice: "without unreasonable delay," subject to law-enforcement and scope-investigation carve-outs.
- Entities holding data on behalf of another owner must notify that owner immediately upon discovery.
- Breaches affecting >1,000 persons at one time: written notice also required to the state Office of Consumer Protection and nationwide consumer reporting agencies.
- **Private right of action exists** — affected individuals may sue for actual damages and attorneys' fees (unusual; most states lack this).

## 5. PCI-DSS Statutory References

- No Hawaii statute found that expressly codifies or references PCI-DSS by name.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes (Beyond Reg E)

- HRS Ch. 490 (Uniform Commercial Code), Article 4A governs wholesale/commercial funds transfers; § 490:4A-108 expressly defers to the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) wherever a transfer is covered by EFTA, and EFTA governs in case of conflict. No Hawaii-specific consumer-facing EFT/ACH statute beyond this UCC Article 4A / EFTA-deference framework was identified.
