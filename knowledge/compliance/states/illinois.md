# Illinois Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Illinois statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding source to ACH-only (no FL-style utility/insurance carve-out identified). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Recurring debit/EFT authorization governed primarily by federal EFTA/Reg E (written/electronic authorization required; revocable any time, 3-business-day notice to stop a specific payment).
- Automatic Contract Renewal Act (815 ILCS 601/1 et seq.) requires consumer consent to the payment mechanism before charging for an auto-renewal term (see §3).

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- Surcharging is permitted; capped at the lesser of 1% or actual processing cost.
- Debit card surcharging prohibited (consistent with nationwide card-network/federal rule).
- Enforced via the Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act, 815 ILCS 505.
- Notice/disclosure of surcharge amount required before the transaction.
- 2026: Illinois Interchange Fee Prohibition Act (IFPA) bars interchange fees on tax/gratuity portions effective 7/1/2026 if merchant informs acquirer of tax/gratuity at authorization/settlement; core provisions upheld by federal district court (Feb 2026) — watch for ongoing litigation.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- Consumer Fraud and Deceptive Business Practices Act — 815 ILCS 505.
- Automatic Contract Renewal Act ("ACRA") — 815 ILCS 601/1 et seq. (effective 2000; consumer contracts only, not B2B):
  - Clear and conspicuous disclosure of auto-renewal terms before purchase.
  - Must obtain consumer consent to auto-renewal terms before charging card/other payment mechanism.
  - Written notice of upcoming renewal 30–60 days before the cancellation deadline, disclosing renewal mechanism and cancellation deadline.
  - Violations = unlawful practice under Consumer Fraud Act; private right of action, actual damages + attorney fees.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), 815 ILCS 530/1 et seq. (effective Jan 1, 2006).
- Covers name + credit/debit card number (or account number with security/access code) among "personal information."
- Notice to affected residents "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay."
- AG notification required if breach affects 500+ Illinois residents.
- Substitute notice allowed if cost >$250,000 or affected group >500,000 residents.
- Law-enforcement delay provision available.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Illinois statute found incorporating or mandating PCI-DSS (unlike NV/WA/MN). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Illinois EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for recurring consumer payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Local Government Acceptance of Credit Cards Act (50 ILCS 345) governs government entities accepting card payments — not general commercial autopay.
