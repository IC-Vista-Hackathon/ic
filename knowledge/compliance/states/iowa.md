# Iowa Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Iowa statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only for utilities/insurance or generally. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Governed by federal EFTA/Reg E for EFT/ACH-funded recurring payments.
- Iowa Consumer Credit Code, Iowa Code § 537.2501, addresses "additional charges" (e.g., bounced-check fees) but does not regulate recurring-payment funding source.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No state-specific statute restricting/prohibiting credit card surcharging — surcharging is legal.
- Debit card surcharging prohibited (nationwide federal rule).
- No state-imposed cap beyond the federal ~4% ceiling driven by card network rules.
- Local governments/public offices may pass through processing costs to customers.
- Iowa Legislature has studied "convenience fees" (see SF 2088 study) but no enacted general private-sector surcharge cap identified. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel for latest session activity.**

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- General consumer fraud statute: Iowa Code § 714.16 — prohibits unfair practices, deception, fraud, false pretense/promise, misrepresentation in connection with sale/lease/advertisement of merchandise; enforced by Iowa AG.
- Iowa Code Chapter 714H — Consumer Frauds, Private Right of Action (Justia listing references § 714H.3).
- No comprehensive automatic-renewal statute for general consumer contracts identified.
- Narrow existing auto-renewal prohibition: Iowa Code § 552.8 — bars automatic-renewal clauses specifically in physical exercise club contracts.
- Other statutory cancellation rights exist for door-to-door sales, buying clubs, funeral services, membership campgrounds, timeshares (separate chapters) — relevant if products bundle these categories.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Personal Information Security Breach Protection Act — Iowa Code Chapter 715C.
- Applies to any person who owns/licenses computerized data with Iowa consumers' personal information used in business/vocation/occupation.
- Notice "in the most expeditious manner possible and without unreasonable delay."
- AG (Consumer Protection Division) written notice required within 5 business days of consumer notice if breach affects 500+ residents.
- No-harm exception: no notice required if, after investigation/law-enforcement consultation, no reasonable likelihood of financial harm is determined and documented (retained 5 years).
- Violations treated as unlawful practices under Iowa's consumer fraud statute (§ 714.16) — AG enforcement.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Iowa statute found incorporating or mandating PCI-DSS compliance. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Iowa EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for general consumer recurring payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
