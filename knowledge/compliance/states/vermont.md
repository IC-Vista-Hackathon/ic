# Vermont Payment Compliance — Internal Reference

Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Credit Card vs ACH
- No Vermont-specific restriction found requiring recurring/autopay to be funded via ACH/bank debit only (no FL-style utility/insurance carve-out identified).
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel if targeting a specific regulated vertical (e.g., insurance, utilities) in VT.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- Surcharging is legal in VT; no VT statute prohibits surcharges.
- Debit card surcharging is not permitted (national card-network rule, not VT-specific statute).
- Minimum purchase amount for credit card transactions capped at $10 — 9 V.S.A. § 2480p.
- State government credit card acceptance/fee provision: 32 V.S.A. § 583.
- No VT-specific surcharge cap statute identified beyond card network rules (~3-4%); confirm current card brand caps separately.

## 3. UDAP / Consumer Protection — Recurring Billing, Negative Option, Autorenewal
- Consumer Fraud Act, 9 V.S.A. §§ 2451–2480g; core prohibition at **9 V.S.A. § 2453(a)** (unfair/deceptive acts or practices in commerce, construed per FTC Act §5(a)(1)).
- Automatic Renewal Provisions in Consumer Contracts — **9 V.S.A. § 2454a**, effective July 1, 2019:
  - Applies to consumer contracts with initial term ≥1 year and renewal term >1 month.
  - Requires clear/conspicuous bold-faced disclosure of renewal terms.
  - Double opt-in: separate affirmative action to accept the auto-renewal term itself (not just the contract).
  - Notice of upcoming renewal required 30–60 days before renewal/termination date.
  - Must provide toll-free number, email, postal address (if directly billing), or other easy cancellation mechanism; online-accepted contracts must allow online-only cancellation.
  - Violation = unfair/deceptive act under § 2453.
  - Exclusions: contracts with financial institutions, credit unions, and insurance contracts.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Security Breach Notice Act, **9 V.S.A. § 2435**.
- Covers computerized personally identifiable information/login credentials, including SSNs, credit/debit card numbers, bank account info.
- Notice to consumers: most expedient time, no unreasonable delay, not later than 45 days after discovery/notification.
- Notice to VT Attorney General: within 14 days of discovery/notification (may be preliminary; kept confidential).
- Exemption if entity determines misuse not reasonably possible and documents that determination to AG/Dept. of Financial Regulation.
- Statute significantly amended effective July 1, 2020.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Vermont statute found incorporating or referencing PCI-DSS compliance requirements (unlike WA RCW 19.255.020).
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel; no hit in AG privacy/data security guidance reviewed.

## 6. State EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone VT EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E identified in this pass.
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel (VT Dept. of Financial Regulation banking statutes may have incidental provisions not reviewed here).
