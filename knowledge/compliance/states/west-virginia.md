# West Virginia Payment Compliance — Internal Reference

Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Credit Card vs ACH
- No West Virginia-specific restriction found requiring recurring/autopay to be funded via ACH/bank debit only (no FL-style utility/insurance carve-out identified).
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel if targeting a specific regulated vertical in WV.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No WV statute specifically restricts private-sector credit card surcharging; secondary sources describe surcharging as permitted, defaulting to federal/card-network rules (~4% cap, no debit/prepaid surcharge).
- **W. Va. Code § 7-5-2A**: authorizes county officers to charge a credit card processing fee/surcharge equal to the actual cost of processing, with mandatory disclosure to payor before the transaction (public-sector fee statute).
- **W. Va. Code R. § 112-12-4** ("Convenience Fees or Service Fees") — state administrative rule addressing convenience/service fees; confirm scope (appears tied to state/county payment processing) directly against the rule text before applying to private commercial transactions.
- § 46A-2-107 (WV Consumer Credit and Protection Act) is unrelated — governs security interests on seller credit cards, not surcharging.
- Not confirmed — no private-sector WV surcharge statute equivalent to WA's RCW 19.200 was identified; verify with counsel.

## 3. UDAP / Consumer Protection — Recurring Billing, Negative Option, Autorenewal
- West Virginia Consumer Credit and Protection Act (WVCCPA), Chapter 46A.
- General UDAP prohibition: **W. Va. Code § 46A-6-104** ("Unfair methods of competition and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in the conduct of any trade or commerce are hereby declared unlawful"), Article 6 (General Consumer Protection). Broadly applied, including to routine consumer transactions.
- Automatic Renewal / Continuous Service Offers: new article **W. Va. Code §§ 46A-6O-1 through 46A-6O-6** (enacted via SB 177):
  - Sets notice/disclosure requirements for automatic purchase renewal and continuous service offers.
  - Prohibits charging a consumer for auto-renewal/continuous service without first obtaining affirmative consent.
  - Requires an accessible cancellation mechanism.
  - § 46A-6O-3 addresses prohibited activities, cancellation mechanisms, and notice of material term changes.
  - Not confirmed — exact effective date and final enacted session (SB 177 text found dated 2024 introduction) not independently verified; confirm current codified/effective status directly at code.wvlegislature.gov before relying on it.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Breach of Security of Consumer Information, **W. Va. Code Chapter 46A, Article 2A** (e.g., § 46A-2A-102 and related sections).
- Applies to entities owning/licensing computerized data including personal information; requires notice to affected WV residents whose unencrypted/unredacted personal information was accessed/acquired without authorization.
- Notice timing: without unreasonable delay, consistent with the time needed to determine breach scope and restore system integrity (no fixed day-count found in this pass — confirm exact deadline language in § 46A-2A-102 et seq. directly).
- Not confirmed — whether payment card numbers are explicitly enumerated as "personal information" under this article should be verified against the statutory definitions section directly.
