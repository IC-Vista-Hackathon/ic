# South Dakota Payment Compliance Reference

Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs ACH
- No South Dakota-specific statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding source (i.e., no SD equivalent of Florida's ACH-only rule for certain recurring utility/insurance billing) — **Not confirmed — verify with counsel**.
- General recurring billing consent/disclosure obligations flow from SD's Deceptive Trade Practices Act (SDCL 37-24) and the FTC Negative Option Rule (incorporated by reference, see §3).

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- South Dakota is **not** one of the states that bans credit card surcharging; surcharging is permitted.
- Common secondary-source guidance: surcharge should not exceed merchant's actual cost of acceptance (network-rule driven, not a distinct statutory cap found in SD code) — **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** for any general statutory surcharge cap on private merchants.
- SDCL 1-8-13.2 caps **convenience fees for payments to state/local government entities** at 3% of the transaction amount (applies to government payment acceptance, not general private-sector surcharging).
- No SD statute found banning surcharges on debit/prepaid distinctly from credit — this distinction in secondary sources is largely a card-network-rule artifact, not codified SD law. **Not confirmed.**

## 3. UDAP / Negative Option / Autorenewal
- SDCL Chapter 37-24 — Deceptive Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Act (SDCL 37-24-1 to 37-24-35).
- SDCL 37-24-6 (subsection addressing negative option plans) incorporates the FTC Negative Option Rule, 16 C.F.R. Part 425, as in effect January 1, 1992, as a deceptive trade practice standard — exact subsection number **not independently confirmed — verify with counsel/statute text**.
- No standalone SD "autorenewal" statute (distinct from California/similar ARL-style laws) identified.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- SDCL 22-40-19 through 22-40-26 (effective July 1, 2018).
- Protected data expressly includes account/credit/debit card number combined with any required security code, access code, password, routing number, or PIN.
- Notice to affected residents required "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," and no later than **60 days** from discovery, subject to law-enforcement delay.
- AG notification required if breach affects **more than 250 SD residents**, within 60 days.
- Enforcement: SD Attorney General action; civil penalty up to **$10,000 per day per violation**, plus fees/costs.

## 5. State PCI-DSS References
- No SD statute found that codifies or cross-references PCI-DSS compliance (unlike NV/MN/WA-style statutes). PCI-DSS obligations arise contractually via card network/processor agreements only. **Confirmed absence based on search — verify with counsel if relying on this.**

## 6. State EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes (beyond federal Reg E)
- SDCL Title 51A, Chapter 17 — Money Transmission Act (licensing for money transmitters/payment processors moving funds on behalf of others).
- SDCL Title 53, Chapter 12 — Electronic Transactions (South Dakota's UETA analog), governs validity of electronic records/signatures.
- No SD-specific consumer EFT/ACH authorization statute beyond Reg E identified. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
