# Kansas Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Kansas statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only for utilities/insurance or generally. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Governed by federal EFTA/Reg E for EFT/ACH-funded recurring payments.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- Kansas historically banned surcharging (1986 no-surcharge statute); struck down as unconstitutional First Amendment restriction in *CardX, LLC v. Schmidt* (federal court).
- Kansas responded with HB 2247 (2024), amending K.S.A. 16a-2-403, effective Jan 1, 2025:
  - Surcharges permitted if clearly and conspicuously disclosed to the customer at point of entry/point of sale and in advance of the transaction.
  - Surcharge must not exceed merchant's actual credit card processing cost.
  - No state-set numeric cap; effective ceiling is the federal/card-network ~4%.
- Statute: K.S.A. § 16a-2-403.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- Kansas Consumer Protection Act (KCPA) — K.S.A. § 50-623 et seq.
- Prohibits deceptive and unconscionable acts in consumer transactions; specifically covers false representations about subscription services, hidden fees, and failure to disclose material auto-renewal terms (billing frequency, cancellation procedure, trial-period limitations).
- No dedicated stand-alone automatic-renewal statute — Kansas is among the states without one; auto-renewal protection is folded into general KCPA deceptive/unconscionable-practices provisions.
- Remedies: actual damages, attorney fees, punitive damages up to $10,000 for willful violations. 3-year statute of limitations from discovery.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Kansas data breach statutes: K.S.A. §§ 50-7a01, 50-7a02, 50-7a04.
- "Personal information" includes first name/initial + last name combined with credit/debit card number, alone or with required security/access code or password.
- Notice "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," accounting for scope/restoration needs.
- Written or electronic notice (E-SIGN compliant) permitted.
- Substitute notice allowed if cost >$100,000, affected individuals >5,000, or insufficient contact info.
- Encrypted/redacted/otherwise-secured data exempted.
- No private right of action; enforcement via civil penalties (AG).

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Kansas statute found incorporating or mandating PCI-DSS compliance (unlike NV/WA/MN). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Kansas EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for general consumer recurring payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
