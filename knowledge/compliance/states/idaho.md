# Idaho — Payment Compliance Reference

*Internal reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Method (Credit Card vs ACH)

- **No Idaho statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH/bank debit over credit card.**
- Idaho Code § 28-42-201 (Idaho Credit Code, Title 28 Ch. 42 — Finance Charges) governs maximum finance charges on consumer credit transactions (loans/credit sales); it does not address or restrict recurring-payment funding method (card vs. ACH).
- Idaho Code § 31-3221 separately authorizes Idaho courts to add electronic-payment convenience fees for card transactions (court-fee context, not general commerce).
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** for insurance-specific premium-finance provisions (Idaho Code Title 41 — Insurance).

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law

- **Idaho has no statute prohibiting or restricting credit card surcharges.** Surcharging is legal in Idaho subject only to federal law and card-network rules (cap/disclosure) — there is no state-law ceiling or ban.

## 3. UDAP / Recurring Billing / Auto-Renewal

- **Idaho Consumer Protection Act** — Idaho Code Title 48, Ch. 6, § 48-603: enumerates unfair methods of competition and unfair/deceptive acts or practices (false sponsorship claims, deceptive representations, misleading practices, price-gouging during declared emergencies, etc.).
- Enforcement: Attorney General action; private right of action under § 48-608 (actual damages or $1,000, whichever is greater).
- **Automatic subscription renewal** — Idaho Code § 48-603G ("Cancellation of Subscriptions"), effective for offers marketed on/after **Jan. 1, 2023**:
  - Defines "automatic subscription renewal" (internet-formed agreements that auto-renew at term end unless cancelled) and "extended automatic subscription renewal" (auto-renews for a term of 12+ months).
  - For extended auto-renewals: seller must give 30–60 day advance notice and provide **at least two cancellation methods, one of which must be free** (notably includes a "click to cancel"-style requirement, similar in spirit to the FTC's 2024 Negative Option Rule though that federal rule was later vacated/stayed — verify current federal-state interplay with counsel).
  - Violations are enforced as Idaho Consumer Protection Act violations (§ 48-603 et seq.).

## 4. Data Breach Notification (Payment Card Data)

- Idaho Code §§ 28-51-104 through 28-51-107 (Title 28, Ch. 51).
- "Personal information" includes SSN, financial account number, or credit/debit card number combined with any required security code/access code/password.
- Covered entities must conduct a reasonable, prompt investigation upon discovering a breach; notice to affected residents required only if misuse has occurred or is reasonably likely.
- Notice timing: "most expedient time possible... without unreasonable delay."
- § 28-51-105(1): **public agencies** must notify the Idaho AG's office within **24 hours** of discovering a breach; commercial entities may notify the AG but are not required to.
- Encryption safe harbor applies.

## 5. PCI-DSS Statutory References

- No Idaho statute found that expressly codifies or references PCI-DSS by name.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes (Beyond Reg E)

- No Idaho-specific consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal Reg E/EFTA identified in this research pass.
