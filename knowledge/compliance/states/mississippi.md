# Mississippi Payment Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific product/flow.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Credit Card vs ACH/Bank Debit
- No MS-specific statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH/bank-debit only (no Florida-style utility/insurance carve-out identified).
- Not confirmed — verify with counsel whether any MS agency rule (Insurance Dept / PSC) imposes a card-vs-ACH restriction on recurring autopay.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No general statewide statute bans private-business credit card surcharging; private merchants may surcharge (subject to federal card-network rules, typically capped around 4%; debit card surcharging is illegal nationwide under network rules).
- **Public-sector restriction**: Miss. Code § 17-25-1 (as amended by SB 2035) prohibits **counties and municipalities** from imposing surcharges/transaction fees on payments made by credit card, charge card, debit card, or other electronic payment.
- Miss. Code § 27-104-33 — governs state agency acceptance of credit/electronic payments; State Dept. of Finance and Administration sets policy allowing fees to be assessed to the user as an additional processing charge for state-agency payments.
- Net effect: **government payments** (state/county/municipal) are surcharge-restricted or agency-fee-governed; **private commercial transactions** are not statutorily restricted.

## 3. UDAP / Negative Option / Autorenewal
- Mississippi Consumer Protection Act (MCPA), Miss. Code Ann. §§ 75-24-1 through 75-24-29; core prohibition at **§ 75-24-5** (unfair methods of competition / unfair or deceptive trade practices — passing off goods, misrepresenting source/sponsorship/approval/affiliation/geographic origin/quality, etc.).
  - Enforcement: AG may seek civil penalty up to $10,000 per knowing/willful violation (§ 75-24-19 per general MCPA enforcement scheme); private right of action for actual damages exists under the MCPA generally.
- **Automatic renewal / negative option**: No comprehensive MS auto-renewal statute is yet in force as a codified consumer right analogous to CA/MN. **SB 2498 (2025 regular session)** — targets subscriptions with renewal terms > 1 month:
  - Requires clear acknowledgment of full auto-renewal terms, cancellation policy, and cancellation instructions at enrollment (with added disclosure if a free trial/gift is involved).
  - Requires a renewal notice 7–21 days before the renewal date via a method the consumer previously agreed to.
  - If a business charges without providing the required acknowledgment/notice, it must fully refund the charge.
  - Consumers who cancel within 10 days after an automatic renewal (for subscriptions > 1 month) are entitled to a pro rata or full refund.
  - Reported effective date July 1, 2025 in bill materials — **confirm final enactment/codification and effective status with counsel** before treating as binding law; bill-tracking sources show this moving through the 2025 session, not yet confirmed as a permanent MS Code section.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Miss. Code Ann. § 75-24-29 (Mississippi Data Breach Notification Law).
- Applies to any person/business conducting business in MS that, in the ordinary course, owns/licenses/maintains computerized personal information of a MS resident.
- Covered "personal information": account number or credit/debit card number combined with any required security code, access code, or password permitting access to the individual's financial account (also covers SSN, driver's license number, etc.).
- Notice to affected individuals required "without reasonable delay" after determining a breach occurred; if 100+ individuals affected, must also notify the MS Attorney General.
- Enforcement: violation constitutes an unfair trade practice, enforced by the AG; **statute states no private right of action** is created.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- Not confirmed — no MS statute equivalent to Minnesota's Plastic Card Security Act was found in this research. Verify with counsel.

## 6. State EFT/ACH-Specific Statutes (beyond federal Reg E)
- Miss. Code Ann. Title 75, Chapter 4A (UCC Article 4A) — § 75-4A-108 addresses the **relationship between UCC Article 4A and the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act** (Article 4A generally yields to EFTA where a transfer is covered by EFTA); this governs wire/funds-transfer interplay rather than establishing an independent MS consumer ACH-authorization regime.
- No standalone MS consumer preauthorized-EFT/ACH statute (comparable to MA ch. 167B or MI Ch. 488) was identified — confirm with counsel if a MS-specific written-authorization requirement is needed beyond federal Reg E / NACHA rules.
- Miss. Code Title 75, Ch. 15 — Mississippi Money Transmitters Act (licensing regime for money transmission, adjacent to but distinct from EFT/ACH consumer-authorization rules).
