# Indiana Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Indiana statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only for utilities/insurance or generally. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Governed by federal EFTA/Reg E for EFT/ACH-funded recurring payments.
- Ind. Code § 24-5-27 (auto-renewal notice statute, see §3) touches payment mechanism disclosure but does not restrict card vs. ACH funding.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No state-level statute restricting or banning credit card surcharging — surcharging is permitted.
- Debit card surcharging prohibited under federal Durbin Amendment (Dodd-Frank), applies nationwide.
- Effective surcharge cap driven by federal/card-network rules (~3–4%), not state law.
- Clear pre-transaction disclosure expected.
- Government-context statutes reference card service fees: Ind. Code § 33-37-6-3 (court fees), § 36-1-8-11 (local government), § 22-12-6-15 (worker's comp payments), § 7.1-3-1-13.5 (alcohol permits) — sector-specific, not general commercial rule.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- Deceptive Consumer Sales Act (DCSA) — Indiana's general UDAP framework; used to address undisclosed auto-renewal/subscription billing practices.
- Ind. Code § 24-5-27 (effective July 1, 2010): limited auto-renewal statute for service contracts (maintenance, repair, sale of real/personal property). Requires written notice ≥30 days before automatic renewal, including renewal date, requirement to notify merchant to terminate, and any fee changes/promotions.
- No comprehensive automatic-renewal law covering all subscription/consumer-goods contracts (repeated legislative attempts have not passed as of last search). **Not confirmed current status — verify with counsel for latest session.**

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Disclosure of Security Breach law — Ind. Code §§ 24-4.9-1 to 24-4.9-5 (effective July 1, 2006).
- "Personal information" includes name + credit/debit card number combined with any required security code/access code/password.
- Notice without unreasonable delay, not more than 45 days after discovery.
- AG notification required within 45 days if any Indiana resident is notified.
- Encryption/redaction safe harbor (no notice required if data encrypted/redacted and key not compromised).
- Civil penalty up to $150,000 per deceptive act; AG may recover investigation costs.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Indiana statute found incorporating or requiring PCI-DSS compliance by law. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Indiana EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for general consumer recurring payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
