# Nebraska — Payments Compliance Reference

*Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any point below.*

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Funding Source (Credit Card vs. ACH)
- No Nebraska-specific statute identified restricting recurring/autopay funding source to ACH/bank debit for any vertical.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- No general state-law prohibition on merchant credit card surcharges — permitted subject to federal/card-network limits (no specific NE percentage cap identified, unlike MT's 3%).
- **Neb. Rev. Stat. § 81-118.01** — state agencies may impose a surcharge/convenience fee for credit/charge card payments, capped at the fee actually charged by the card company/merchant bank, and only when permitted by card network rules or authorized in writing by the card company.
- **Neb. Rev. Stat. § 13-609** — parallel provision for county treasurers/political subdivisions; same cap/authorization conditions. Also separately addresses (per statute title) prohibition on political subdivision acceptance of central bank digital currency.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option
- **Nebraska Consumer Protection Act** — Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 59-1601 to 59-1623. Declares unfair methods of competition/unfair or deceptive acts or practices in trade or commerce unlawful (§ 59-1602); uncapped damages, fee-shifting for willful violations.
- **Uniform Deceptive Trade Practices Act** — Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 87-301 et seq. (§ 87-303 — damages, injunction, costs). Recognizes 18 enumerated deceptive trade practices; injunctive relief does not require proof of monetary damage or intent.
- Automatic renewal: **No enacted general automatic-renewal statute confirmed.** LB 132 ("Automatic Renewal Limitation Act," 108th Legislature, 2023) would have required clear/conspicuous disclosure and affirmative consumer assent, with civil penalties up to $2,500/violation — **indefinitely postponed April 18, 2024; did not become law.** Recurring billing/negative-option practices remain governed only by the general UDAP/UDTPA statutes above.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- **Financial Data Protection and Consumer Notification of Data Security Breach Act of 2006** — Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 87-801 to 87-808.
- "Personal information" includes name + credit/debit card number in combination with a required security code/PIN/password/other means of access (also covers routing-code + security-code combos, biometric data, and username/email + password).
- Entity must conduct a "reasonable and prompt investigation" on discovery of a breach; notify affected NE residents "as soon as possible and without unreasonable delay" if unauthorized use occurred/is reasonably likely.
- AG must receive notice **at the same time** as affected individuals (no minimum consumer-count threshold identified for AG notice — differs from states with a numeric trigger).
- Enforcement is via the Nebraska Consumer Protection Act: civil penalties up to **$2,000 per violation**; AG subpoena power; recovery of direct economic damages for affected residents.

## 5. PCI-DSS Reference in Statute
- No Nebraska statute identified that mandates or references PCI-DSS compliance (contrast with Nevada NRS 603A.215). State agencies accepting cards must complete an annual PCI SAQ reviewed by an internal ISA and submitted to the State Treasurer's Office — an internal state-government requirement, not a statute binding private merchants.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes
- No standalone Nebraska consumer-facing EFT/ACH statute beyond adoption of federal EFTA framework; Nebraska statutes on EFT (e.g., § 13-609, Banking Act Ch. 8) chiefly address government/institutional payment acceptance, not consumer recurring-debit protections.
