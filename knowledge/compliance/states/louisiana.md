# Louisiana Payment Compliance Reference

> Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice. Verify with counsel before acting.

## 1. Autopay/Recurring Payment Authorization — Card vs. ACH Restrictions
- No Louisiana statute found restricting recurring/autopay funding to ACH-only (vs. credit card) for utilities, insurance, or generally. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Governed by federal EFTA/Reg E for EFT/ACH-funded recurring payments.
- La. R.S. 9:2716 (auto-renewal disclosure, see §3) governs contract terms/cancellation, not funding-method restrictions.

## 2. Credit Card Surcharge / Convenience Fee Law
- Credit card surcharging is legal in Louisiana; commonly cited practical cap ~4% (federal/card-network driven, not a Louisiana-specific statutory cap — verify current statutory text).
- **Debit card surcharge prohibition enacted 2026**: Act 751 (2026, SB 254), codified at La. R.S. 51:3081–3082 (new Title 51, Chapter 52), prohibits retail businesses from surcharging customers who pay by debit card vs. cash/check/credit/other similar means. Effective August 1, 2026.
- La. R.S. 33:2933 addresses credit/debit card payment authorizations, contracts, and fees in a specific (local government) context — confirm scope with counsel if relevant to public-sector billing.

## 3. UDAP / Autorenewal / Negative-Option Statutes
- Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law (UTPCPL) — La. R.S. 51:1401 et seq.
  - R.S. 51:1405: prohibits "unfair methods of competition and unfair or deceptive acts or practices" in trade/commerce.
  - R.S. 51:1409: private right of action for ascertainable loss; treble damages if AG had put defendant on notice and violation was knowing; attorney fees recoverable; 1-year liberative prescription (statute of limitations) from the transaction/act.
- Automatic renewal contract disclosure — La. R.S. 9:2716: requires clear/conspicuous disclosure of automatic renewal clause in the contract/offer, and clear/conspicuous disclosure of how to cancel.
- Louisiana "Click-to-Cancel Act" — enacted via 2026 Regular Session HB 750 (see enrolled text), adds negative-option/cancellation requirements within Title 51; violations treated as unfair/deceptive trade practices. **Confirm final codified section numbers and effective date with counsel** — search results show enrolled bill but not yet a stable R.S. citation at time of review.

## 4. Data Breach Notification — Payment Card Data
- Database Security Breach Notification Law — La. R.S. 51:3071–3077.
- Covers "personal information" including account number, credit/debit card number combined with required security code/access code/password permitting account access.
- Notification to affected residents required within 60 days of discovering breach; written notice to Louisiana AG within 10 days of resident notifications.
- Applies to any person conducting business in LA and owning/licensing computerized personal data, government agencies, and third-party data maintainers.
- Penalty: fine up to $5,000 per violation for untimely notice.

## 5. State Law Referencing/Incorporating PCI-DSS
- No Louisiana statute found that independently codifies or mandates PCI-DSS compliance by law. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- PCI-DSS in Louisiana is a contractual obligation (card networks/processors), not a statutory requirement.

## 6. State-Specific EFT/ACH Statutes Beyond Reg E
- No standalone Louisiana consumer EFT/ACH statute beyond federal EFTA/Reg E identified for private recurring payments. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Louisiana Consumer Credit Law (Title 9, various) touches electronic debits/returned payments in the credit context but is not a general EFT/ACH consumer-protection statute.
