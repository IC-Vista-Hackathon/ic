# South Dakota — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially those flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** SDCL §§ 22-40-19 to 22-40-26 ("Protection of Personal or Protected Information," effective July 1, 2018).
- **Trigger:** "Breach of system security" = unauthorized acquisition of unencrypted computerized data, or encrypted computerized data plus the encryption key, that materially compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal or protected information maintained by an "information holder." SDCL § 22-40-19, § 22-40-20.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** Not later than **60 days** from discovery/notification of the breach, unless a longer period is needed for legitimate law enforcement needs. SDCL § 22-40-20.
- **State/CRA notification threshold:** If breach affects **more than 250 SD residents**, must also notify the SD Attorney General (mail or email) and, per general practice, consumer reporting agencies. SDCL § 22-40-21 (AG notice provision — confirm exact subsection with counsel).
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** **Yes.** "Personal information" = first name/initial + last name combined with account, credit card, or debit card number **in combination with any required security code, access code, password, routing number, PIN, or other information permitting access to a financial account.** SDCL § 22-40-19.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — encrypted data is excluded from the breach definition, **unless the encryption key was also compromised.** "Encrypted" defined per FIPS 140-2 (as in effect 1/1/2018) or functionally equivalent rendering data unreadable. SDCL § 22-40-19.
- **Penalties:** Violation = deceptive act/practice under SD's Deceptive Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Act; AG may seek civil penalties up to **$10,000 per day per violation**, plus attorneys' fees/costs. SDCL § 22-40-26.
- **Private right of action:** **Not confirmed — no PRA identified in statute text reviewed; enforcement appears AG-only.** Verify with counsel.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Price increase / recurring charge notice (general):** **Not confirmed** — no general SD statute located requiring advance notice before a price increase or ordinary recurring charge outside specific regulated industries (e.g., insurance, utilities).
- **Automatic renewal / autopay notice:** SD's automatic-renewal protections are **narrow and sector-specific** (e.g., health club/dance studio contracts, certain service contracts, equipment leases, buyers' clubs) rather than a general Automatic Renewal Law (ARL) like CA/TX. Exact codification (candidate: SDCL ch. 37-24 consumer protection / SDCL ch. 58-17E for a specific sector) **could not be confirmed** from available sources — verify current chapter/section with counsel before relying on a specific cite.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** **Not confirmed** as a generally-applicable statutory requirement; likely exists only within the narrow sector-specific contracts described above.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Not confirmed.** No SD-specific statute was identified imposing notice requirements for preauthorized EFTs/recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005) / EFTA. Treat federal Reg E §1005.10 (10-day notice for varying-amount preauthorized transfers, written authorization requirement) as the operative standard for SD consumers absent a state-specific overlay.
