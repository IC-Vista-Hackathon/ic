# Utah — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially those flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Utah Code § 13-44-101 et seq. (Protection of Personal Information Act); notice provision § 13-44-202; definitions § 13-44-102. Updated by SB 98, effective **May 1, 2024** (added AG/Utah Cyber Center notice).
- **Trigger:** Person who owns/licenses computerized data including personal information on a Utah resident, upon becoming aware of a breach of system security, must conduct a good-faith, reasonable, and prompt investigation to determine likelihood personal information has been/will be misused for identity theft or fraud. Utah Code § 13-44-202.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **No fixed day count** — notice required "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," accounting for investigation scope and law enforcement needs. Utah Code § 13-44-202.
- **State AG / Cyber Center notification threshold:** Required if breach affects **500 or more Utah residents** (post-SB 98, 2024). Utah Code § 13-44-202 (as amended).
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** **Yes.** "Personal information" = first name/initial + last name combined with (among other elements) a financial account number or credit/debit card number **and any required security code, access code, or password permitting access to the account**, when the name or data element is unencrypted or not otherwise rendered unreadable. Utah Code § 13-44-102.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — the PI definition itself excludes data that is encrypted or otherwise protected by a method rendering it unreadable/unusable; standard construction implies the safe harbor fails if the means to render it readable was also compromised (**exact statutory carve-out language not independently confirmed — verify with counsel**).
- **Penalties:** Enforced by the Utah AG; civil penalties **up to $2,500 per consumer**, **capped at $100,000 aggregate** for related violations arising from a single breach.
- **Private right of action:** **Not confirmed** — no PRA identified in the statute text reviewed; enforcement appears AG-only.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Statute:** Utah Code Title 13, **Chapter 70** ("Automatic Renewal Contracts Act"), effective **January 1, 2025** (enacted via HB 174, 2024); notice provision at § 13-70-201.
- **Scope:** Applies to contracts that automatically renew at the end of a defined paid period for a subsequent paid term of **more than 45 days**.
- **Advance renewal notice:** Business must send notice **at least 30 but not more than 60 days before** the automatic renewal date, clearly and conspicuously disclosing the renewal terms (price, term, how to cancel). Utah Code § 13-70-201.
- **Trial-period offers:** Separately addressed in the same section — clear notice requirements apply before a paid term begins following a trial. Utah Code § 13-70-201.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Act requires clear and conspicuous cancellation mechanisms; a specific "confirmation of cancellation" notice content requirement **not confirmed** — verify current text of Chapter 70 Part 2.
- **Penalties:** Enforced via administrative/court order; civil penalty **up to $5,000 per violation** for violating such an order.
- **Price-increase notice (general, non-renewal context):** **Not confirmed** — no general Utah statute located requiring advance notice of an ordinary price increase outside the auto-renewal/trial-period context above.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Not confirmed as consumer-notice-specific.** Utah Code Title 70A, Chapter 4A (UCC — Funds Transfers) governs wholesale/commercial funds transfers and **expressly does not apply** to transfers governed by the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act (Utah Code § 70A-4a-108); where Title 70A-4a and the EFTA both could apply (e.g., remittance transfers), federal law controls. No separate Utah statute was identified imposing consumer notice requirements for preauthorized EFT/recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). Utah Code Title 7-16a (Financial Institutions Act) references payment-related definitions but its content on consumer preauthorized-transfer notice **could not be confirmed** from sources reviewed — verify directly before relying on it.
