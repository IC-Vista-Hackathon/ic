# Kansas — Notification Law Reference

> **Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice.** Verify all citations with counsel before relying on them for a specific incident or contract decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** Consumer Information; Security Breach, K.S.A. 50-7a01 et seq.

- **Trigger:** Unauthorized access to and acquisition of unencrypted or un-redacted computerized personal information that compromises its security, confidentiality, or integrity, and that causes (or the entity reasonably believes has caused or will cause) identity theft to a consumer — confirmed via a good-faith investigation. K.S.A. 50-7a01, 50-7a02.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — notice required "as soon as possible," in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay, consistent with legitimate law-enforcement needs. K.S.A. 50-7a02.
- **State AG notice:** Not confirmed as a standalone mandatory trigger in the sections reviewed — Kansas's notable notice obligation runs to **consumer reporting agencies**, not a direct mandatory AG filing threshold. Verify current statute text with counsel, as some multi-state summaries note this differently.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Required if the breach requires notifying **more than 1,000 consumers at one time**; notice to all nationwide CRAs must be given without unreasonable delay. K.S.A. 50-7a02.
- **Payment card number + security code as "personal information":** **Yes.** Definition includes a financial account, credit, or debit card number, alone or in combination with any required security code, access code, or password permitting account access. K.S.A. 50-7a01.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** **Yes.** The breach definition itself is limited to "unencrypted or un-redacted" data; encrypted data (via a defined algorithmic-transformation standard) falls outside the trigger. K.S.A. 50-7a01.
- **Penalties:** Attorney General may bring an action in law or equity to address violations (except as to insurance companies, which are handled separately) and obtain appropriate relief. K.S.A. 50-7a02. No fixed statutory civil-penalty dollar amount was identified — **not confirmed, verify with counsel.**
- **Private right of action:** Not confirmed — no private right of action was found in the sections reviewed; enforcement runs through the Attorney General.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **No general automatic-renewal statute identified for Kansas.** Multiple secondary sources confirm Kansas has not enacted an auto-renewal/subscription-notice law (legislation was introduced in 2017 but did not pass into a comprehensive statute).
- Kansas Consumer Protection Act, K.S.A. Ch. 50, Art. 6, includes general unfair/deceptive-practices provisions (e.g., K.S.A. 50-640 on door-to-door sales cancellation rights) but nothing specific to price-increase notice, autopay/subscription renewal notice, or cancellation-confirmation notice was located.
- **Conclusion:** For Kansas, rely on the federal FTC "click-to-cancel"/negative-option framework and general KCPA unfair-practices theory rather than a state-specific notice-timing statute. **Not confirmed as exhaustive — verify with counsel.**

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Kansas-specific statute imposing consumer notice requirements for preauthorized transfers or recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E was identified.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** before relying on the absence of a Kansas-specific EFT notice statute.
