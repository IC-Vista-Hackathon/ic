# Maryland — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Maryland Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), Md. Code, Com. Law §§ 14-3501 to 14-3508.
- **Trigger:** A business that owns, licenses, or maintains computerized PI of a Maryland resident discovers or is notified of a breach of the security of a system; must conduct a good-faith, reasonable, and prompt investigation to determine likelihood of misuse.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** As soon as reasonably practicable, but **not later than 45 days** after the business discovers or is notified of the breach.
- **AG notice:** Required **before** notifying affected individuals — notice to the Maryland Office of the Attorney General must precede consumer notice, including number of affected MD residents, breach description, remediation steps, and a sample of the consumer notice. No separate fixed day-count for the AG notice beyond "as soon as practicable" ahead of consumer notice.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** **Yes** — Com. Law § 14-3501(e) covers "an account number, a credit card number, or a debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password," alongside SSN/ITIN/passport/federal ID, driver's license/state ID, health/health-insurance data, biometric and genetic data, and email address + password/security-question answers.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — data elements are excluded from "personal information" if "encrypted, redacted, or otherwise protected by another method that renders the information unreadable or unusable."
- **Penalties:** AG may issue a cease-and-desist order and levy civil penalties — **up to $1,000 for a first violation**, **up to $5,000 for subsequent violations.**
- **Private right of action:** **Yes** — consumers may sue to recover injuries/losses and may recover attorneys' fees. (Distinct from Maryland's newer comprehensive privacy law, MODPA, which has **no** private right of action and is AG-enforcement-only with penalties up to $10,000/violation, $25,000 for repeated violations — do not conflate PIPA breach-notice duties with MODPA.)

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **New automatic renewal law:** HB 107 / SB 49 (2025 Regular Session, Ch. 204/205), signed **April 22, 2025**, **effective June 1, 2026.**
  - **Cancellation:** Must allow cancellation in a manner at least as easy as the sign-up method (online, email, or phone).
  - **Renewal notice window:** For contracts with an **initial term of 1 year or more**, notice required **at least 15 days and no more than 45 days before renewal.**
  - **Trial/discount period conversion notice:** Notice required **before a free trial or discounted introductory period exceeding 14 days converts to a paid subscription** (reported figure: 14 days' advance notice of conversion — confirm exact day-count against enrolled bill text; source PDF was not machine-readable during this research pass). **Not fully confirmed — verify exact day-count with counsel/enrolled bill text.**
  - **Violation treatment:** A violation is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practice under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act.
  - **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a distinct standalone requirement from sources reviewed. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- **Prior/older auto-renewal provision:** Com. Law § 14-12B-06 (auto-renewal clauses in certain consumer contracts) predates HB 107/SB 49 and addresses consumer cancellation rights and required disclosure formatting; relationship/overlap with the new 2026 law **not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Maryland-specific statute was found imposing preauthorized-transfer or recurring-debit change-in-terms notice requirements beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
