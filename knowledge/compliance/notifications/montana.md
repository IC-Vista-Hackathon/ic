# Montana — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice — verify with counsel before relying on this for a live incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Mont. Code Ann. § 30-14-1704 (Computer security breach)
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of computerized data that materially compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal information *and* causes or is reasonably believed to cause loss or injury to a Montana resident.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — "without unreasonable delay," consistent with law enforcement needs and time needed to determine scope/restore system integrity.
- **AG notice:** Required — must simultaneously submit an electronic copy of the consumer notification plus date/method of distribution to the Montana AG's Office of Consumer Protection (no consumer count threshold specified; excludes info that personally identifies notice recipients).
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Not confirmed in this section — no CRA-notice provision found comparable to MO/NV's 1,000-person trigger.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** Yes — "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" permitting account access is explicitly personal information.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — encrypted personal information is excluded from the definition triggering disclosure.
- **Penalties:** Not confirmed in § 30-14-1704 itself — verify with counsel on enforcement mechanism/amount (may run through general Consumer Protection Act unfair-practices remedies).
- **Private right of action:** Not confirmed — not addressed in this section; verify with counsel.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Utility/natural gas auto-renewal notice:** ARM 38.5.6004 (Small Customer Service Contract, natural gas restructuring) — supplier must give written notice **at least 60 days** before contract expiration disclosing either (a) the existence/operation of an auto-renewal clause, or (b) the need to affirmatively renew. If the contract auto-renews, any change in terms/conditions at renewal requires **written notice at least 60 days in advance**.
  - Scope is limited to small-customer natural gas service contracts — not a general consumer subscription/autopay statute. Do not extrapolate to payments/billing SaaS products without counsel review.
- **General price-increase / recurring-charge / autopay notice statute:** Not confirmed — no general Montana ARL (automatic renewal law) analogous to CA found for consumer subscriptions outside the utility context above.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no statute found.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Montana-specific EFT/ACH notice statute found beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 205, incl. § 1005.10 preauthorized transfers).
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** for any Montana Division of Banking and Financial Institutions guidance beyond Reg E.
