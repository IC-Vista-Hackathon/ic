# Connecticut — Notification Law Reference

**Not legal advice.** Internal compliance reference only; verify with counsel before relying on any item below.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 36a-701b.

- **Trigger:** Breach of security of computerized data containing personal information of a CT resident.
- **Deadline to consumers:** Without unreasonable delay, **not later than 60 days** after discovery of the breach, unless a shorter federal deadline applies.
- **AG notice:** Required **not later than the time notice is provided to residents** (i.e., no separate/longer grace period — must be simultaneous or earlier).
- **Consumer reporting agencies:** No statutory requirement to notify CRAs found in § 36a-701b.
- **Payment card + security code covered?** **Yes** — "credit or debit card number" combined with "any required security code, access code or password" is included as qualifying personal information.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — applies only where PI "has not been secured by encryption or by any other method or technology that renders the personal information unreadable or unusable."
- **Identity theft services:** Distinctive CT requirement — must offer affected residents identity theft prevention/mitigation services at no cost for **not less than 2 years**.
- **Penalties:** Violation constitutes an **unfair trade practice** under CUTPA; civil penalties reportedly up to **$5,000 per willful violation** (secondary-source figure — **verify exact CUTPA penalty amount with counsel**).
- **Private right of action:** **No** — enforcement rests exclusively with the CT Attorney General.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statutes:** Conn. Gen. Stat. § 42-126b (trial/introductory offers, automatic renewals — unfair trade practice) and Chapter 742d (Automatic Renewal and Continuous Services).

- **Autopay/subscription renewal notice:**
  - Contracts **>180 days** with auto-renewal for **>31 days**: clear and conspicuous written notice of cancellation right/procedure required **15–60 days** before the renewal date or expiration of the cancellation period, whichever is earlier.
  - Contracts **≤180 days** with auto-renewal for **>31 days**: cancellation-rights notice and procedure must be **in the contract itself**, with the consumer given up to **60 days** before expiration to cancel.
- **Consequence of non-compliance:** Goods/services provided after the scheduled contract end are deemed an **unconditional gift** with no consumer obligation to pay, if the required notice was omitted.
- **Price increase notice:** No separate statutory price-increase-notice requirement identified beyond the renewal-notice regime above — **verify with counsel** whether Chapter 742d imposes additional material-change notice duties.
- **Cancellation confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a distinct requirement — verify with counsel.
- **Enforcement:** Violation is an unfair trade practice (CUTPA) — AG enforcement; CUTPA generally does carry a private right of action for unfair trade practices broadly (distinct from the breach-notice statute above) — **verify applicability to auto-renewal violations specifically with counsel.**

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Connecticut-specific statute imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice requirements beyond federal Reg E was identified in this research. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel**, particularly under Title 36a (Banking) provisions on electronic transfers.
