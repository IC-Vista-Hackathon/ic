# Vermont — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Security Breach Notice Act, 9 V.S.A. §§ 2430, 2435.
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of computerized "personally identifiable information" (PII) or login credentials by an unauthorized person. Data collector must notify following discovery or notification of the breach.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** "Most expedient time possible... but not later than 45 days after discovery or notification," unless law enforcement delay applies.
- **AG / regulator notice:**
  - Attorney General or Department of Financial Regulation (for DFR-regulated entities, e.g., insurers, banks): preliminary notice within **14 business days** of discovery/reasonable belief that ≥1 Vermont resident affected. No minimum-size threshold — all breaches require AG notice.
  - Consumer reporting agencies: "without unreasonable delay" if breach affects **1,000+** consumers (exemption for financial institutions already regulated re: breach notice).
- **Payment card + security code covered as PII:** Not confirmed from the text of § 2435 itself — § 2430(10) contains the substantive PII definition and was not fully retrieved; Vermont's PII definition is understood industry-wide to include financial account/card number + required security code (per secondary sources), but **verify § 2430(10)(A) directly with counsel** before relying on this for a specific incident.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** None found in the statute text reviewed — Vermont does not appear to exempt encrypted data from the "PII" trigger the way many other states do. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
- **Penalties:** AG enforcement; secondary sources cite penalties up to **$10,000 per violation** (civil, under Vermont's Consumer Protection Act enforcement authority). Not independently verified against statutory text — verify with counsel.
- **Private right of action:** None. Enforcement vested in AG, State's Attorney, and DFR only.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Automatic renewal law:** 9 V.S.A. § 2454a (effective July 1, 2019) — one of the strictest in the country.
  - Applies to consumer contracts with an **initial term ≥ 1 year** that auto-renew for a term **longer than 1 month**.
  - Requires: (1) clear/conspicuous bold-face disclosure of the auto-renewal term; (2) a **separate affirmative opt-in** to the auto-renewal clause, distinct from acceptance of the contract itself; (3) **written or electronic notice 30–60 days before renewal**.
  - Financial institutions and credit unions (as defined under Vermont law) are **exempt**.
  - Violations are treated as unfair/deceptive trade practices under 9 V.S.A. § 2453 (Vermont Consumer Protection Act) — meaning private right of action and AG enforcement both available under the CPA (litigation trend noted by Vermont practitioners).
- **Price-increase-specific notice / recurring-charge notice outside auto-renewal:** Not confirmed — no separate statute identified beyond § 2454a's renewal-notice requirement.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice requirement:** Not explicitly separately mandated beyond the § 2454a disclosure/cancellation-mechanism requirements. Verify with counsel for specific product designs.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- No standalone Vermont consumer-EFT statute was identified that imposes obligations beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR 1005). Search results describing a "3 business days to stop a preauthorized transfer" and "10 days' notice for varying-amount payments" rule reflect **federal Reg E §1005.10**, not distinct Vermont statutory text — do not cite these as Vermont-specific requirements without further verification.
- Vermont's own funds-transfer provisions (8 V.S.A. ch. 79, relating to money services) primarily govern money transmitters/licensing, not consumer EFT notice rights, and defer to the federal EFTA where inconsistent.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether any Vermont-specific EFT consumer-notice rule exists beyond Reg E incorporation.
