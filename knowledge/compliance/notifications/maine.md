# Maine — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Notice of Risk to Personal Data Act, 10 M.R.S. §§ 1346–1350-B (Title 10, Chapter 210-B).
- **Trigger:** Any business, government agency, or institution that maintains computerized personal information experiences a breach of security involving unencrypted/unredacted PI.
- **Consumer + regulator notice deadline:** Notification to affected ME residents **and** to the Attorney General (or the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation for regulated entities) required **within 30 days** of discovering the breach and determining its scope — one of the strictest fixed windows among the states.
- **Covered entities:** Broadly worded — information brokers, private businesses, government agencies, municipalities, school districts, and universities all fall within scope.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** **Yes.** 10 M.R.S. § 1347(6) covers "account number, credit card number or debit card number, if circumstances exist wherein such a number could be used without additional identifying information, access codes or passwords," and separately lists "account passwords or personal identification numbers or other access codes" as a standalone covered element.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — "encryption" (disguising data via generally accepted practices) or redaction takes the data outside the PI definition.
- **Other safe harbor:** Entities already subject to and compliant with a federal or state breach-notification regime that is "at least as protective" as § 1348 (e.g., HIPAA, GLBA) are deemed compliant with Maine's law.
- **Penalties:** Civil penalties up to **$500 per violation**, capped at **$2,500 per day** of continued violation. Government entities and public educational institutions are exempt from monetary penalties.
- **Private right of action:** Not created — § 1347 (definitions) and the enforcement provisions reviewed contain no individual private right of action; enforcement runs through the AG/civil penalty structure. **Not confirmed for the full chapter — verify with counsel.**

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Automatic subscription renewal law:** 10 M.R.S. § 1210-C (Title 10, Chapter 205-B — Automatic Subscription Renewal).
  - **Renewal notice window:** Seller may not auto-renew a subscription unless it gives the consumer **clear and conspicuous notice no less than 30 days and no more than 60 days before the cancellation deadline.**
  - **Extended/12-month+ subscriptions:** Separate notice required **no more than 30 days before the renewal date** stating that the subscription will auto-renew unless cancelled.
  - **Cancellation mechanism:** Sellers must provide an easily accessible disclosure of cancellation methods **and** an online cancellation method available via the same computer network used to sign up.
  - **2026 amendment (SP 650):** Requires **separate consumer consent** to the automatic-renewal provision itself (not bundled into general contract acceptance); applies to agreements entered into or renewed **on or after January 1, 2026.**
  - **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a distinct standalone requirement in the sections reviewed. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- **Price-increase notice tied to renewal:** The 30–60 day pre-cancellation-deadline notice functionally covers price changes tied to renewal, but a standalone mid-term price-increase notice statute was not identified. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- Separately, Maine has a subscription/membership cancellation statute at 10 M.R.S. § 1210-C referenced above; a related provision, § 1013 (as sometimes cited), was **not located** under that number in the current statutes — treat any reference to "§1013" as unconfirmed.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Maine-specific statute was found imposing preauthorized-transfer or recurring-debit change-in-terms notice requirements beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
