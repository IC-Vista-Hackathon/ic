# Alaska — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Alaska Personal Information Protection Act, AS 45.48.010–45.48.090.
- **Trigger:** Breach of security of an information system containing personal information of an Alaska resident (unauthorized acquisition compromising security/confidentiality). AS 45.48.010.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** "Most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," after discovery or notification of the breach; no fixed day-count in statute (unlike AL/AR/AZ's 45-day rule). May be delayed if the Alaska Department of Law determines disclosure would compromise an investigation. AS 45.48.010/.020.
- **AG/state agency notice:** No dollar/individual-count trigger for notifying the Department of Law directly written into the base disclosure section; the Department of Law can independently direct delay of consumer notice. Not confirmed — verify current text of AS 45.48.040 for any state-agency notice trigger before relying.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Required, without unreasonable delay, if the breach requires notifying **more than 1,000** Alaska residents. Notice to nationwide CRAs must include timing, distribution, and content of the resident notices. AS 45.48.040.
- **Payment card + security code as PII:** Not independently confirmed in full statutory text during this research pass — Alaska's definition (AS 45.48.090) is understood to follow the common state pattern (name + financial account/card number in combination with a required security code/access code/PIN). Not confirmed — verify exact definitional text before relying for a coverage determination.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Not confirmed from primary text in this pass; secondary sources consistently describe Alaska's law as applying only to unencrypted personal information (encryption functions as a safe harbor), consistent with the near-universal state pattern. Verify AS 45.48.090 directly with counsel.
- **Penalties:**
  - Government agency violator: civil penalty up to **$500 per unnotified resident**, capped at **$50,000** total.
  - Non-government violator: violation is treated as an unfair/deceptive act or practice under AS 45.50.471–.561, but is **not** subject to the standard UDAP civil penalties — instead liable to the state for up to **$500 per unnotified resident**, capped at **$50,000** total. AS 45.48.080.
- **Private right of action:** A person harmed by a breach may bring a civil action for **actual economic damages, capped at $500**, plus costs and attorney's fees. This is a limited private right of action (unusual among these four states — AL/AZ/AR do not provide one).

## 2. Consumer Payment/Billing Notices

- **Auto-renewal / recurring charge / subscription-renewal notice law:** **No general Alaska auto-renewal statute.** Alaska has enacted a narrow, sector-specific law (AS 45.45.940 area) governing automatic renewal of **home security product/service contracts** only: requires a separate disclosure document, and if the consumer does not sign to accept auto-renewal, requires notice of scheduled termination **at least 60 days** before the termination date. Not confirmed — verify enactment/effective date of the underlying bill (tracked as SB103) directly with counsel; no general subscription/recurring-charge notice law exists beyond this sector.
- **Price-increase notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no general statute located.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no general statute located (home-security sector law above requires termination-notice content, not a broader cancellation-confirmation duty).

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Alaska consumer-facing statute was found imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice obligations beyond federal Regulation E. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
