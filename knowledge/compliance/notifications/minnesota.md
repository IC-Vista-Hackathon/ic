# Minnesota — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification — Minn. Stat. § 325E.61 (related: § 13.055)

- **Statute cite:** Minn. Stat. § 325E.61.
- **Trigger:** Any person/business conducting business in MN that owns or licenses data including personal information must disclose any breach of security following discovery/notification of the breach, to any MN resident whose unencrypted personal information was, or is reasonably believed to have been, acquired by an unauthorized person.
- **Deadline to notify consumers:** No fixed day count — **"in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay,"** consistent with legitimate law-enforcement needs and measures necessary to determine breach scope, identify affected individuals, and restore system integrity.
- **Consumer reporting agency (CRA) notice:** **Fixed 48-hour deadline** — if notification is required to more than 500 persons at one time, the entity must also notify, **within 48 hours**, all nationwide consumer reporting agencies (referencing § 13.055, subd. 6 for the mechanics). This is the one hard numeric deadline in the MN statute.
- **State agency/AG notice:** No separate mandatory AG pre-notice trigger identified beyond the AG's enforcement role under Minn. Stat. § 8.31. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** **Yes, explicitly.** Covered data = first name/initial + last name combined with SSN, driver's license/MN ID number, or "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password."
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. Data secured by encryption or other technology rendering it unreadable is excluded from the notice trigger — **unless** the encryption key/password was also compromised.
- **Penalties:** AG enforces under § 8.31; civil penalties up to **$25,000 per action**.
- **Private right of action:** **Yes** — Minnesota's statute is one of the few with an explicit private right of action, allowing affected individuals to bring their own suit for damages and attorney fees, separate from AG enforcement. Any waiver of § 325E.61 protections is void as against public policy.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices — Minn. Stat. §§ 325G.56–325G.59 (Automatic Renewal / Continuous Service)

- **Definitions (§ 325G.56):** "Automatic renewal" = subscription/purchasing agreement automatically renewed at the end of a definite term for a subsequent term. "Continuous service" = agreement that continues until the consumer terminates it. "Offer terms" must disclose: that the agreement continues until termination, cancellation policy, recurring charges and potential changes, term length/continuous-service status, and any minimum-purchase obligation.
- **Reminder/renewal notice timing (§ 325G.57):** Renewal notices must be sent **no earlier than 30 days and no later than 15 days before renewal** (acceptable delivery: email, mail, hand delivery); a final renewal reminder is required **at least 3 days prior** to the renewal date. Notices must clearly state the renewal date, terms, and cancellation instructions.
- **Annual reminder for ongoing/indefinite subscriptions:** For continuous-service agreements without a fixed renewal date, businesses must send a written reminder **at least once per calendar year**, covering the terms of service and how to terminate/manage it, delivered in the same medium that activated the automatic renewal/continuous service (or the medium the consumer is accustomed to using with the business).
- **Material-change notice:** Upon a material change to the terms of an indefinite subscription agreement, the seller must give the consumer **clear and conspicuous notice in a timely manner, and in any case before implementing the change**, along with instructions on how to terminate, in a form the consumer can retain.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not separately identified as a distinct numeric-deadline requirement in the sections reviewed. Not confirmed — verify with counsel (§ 325G.58/.59 may contain additional cancellation-mechanism requirements worth a follow-up pull).
