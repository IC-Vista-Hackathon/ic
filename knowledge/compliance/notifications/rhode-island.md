# Rhode Island — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Identity Theft Protection Act of 2015, **R.I. Gen. Laws § 11-49.3-1 et seq.** (notice at **§ 11-49.3-4**; penalties at **§ 11-49.3-5**).
- **Trigger:** Disclosure of personal information, or breach of security, that poses a **significant risk of identity theft** to an RI resident whose personal information was, or is reasonably believed to have been, acquired by an unauthorized person/entity.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **45 calendar days** after confirmation of the breach for non-government persons/businesses; **30 calendar days** for state/municipal agencies.
- **AG / CRA notice:** If **more than 500** RI residents must be notified, must also notify the RI Attorney General and major consumer reporting agencies as to timing, content, and distribution — without delaying consumer notice.
- **"Personal information" includes payment card + code?** Yes — "account number, credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, password, or personal identification number, that would permit access to an individual's financial account."
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. Encrypted or hard-copy data is excluded from "personal information," but data is **not** considered encrypted (i.e., safe harbor is lost) if acquired together with the key, security code, or password needed to access it.
- **Penalties:** Civil penalty — up to **$100 per record** for reckless violations; up to **$200 per record** for knowing and willful violations. Enforcement is by the RI Attorney General, who "may bring an action in the name of the state."
- **Private right of action:** **No** — § 11-49.3-5 grants enforcement exclusively to the Attorney General.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **Statute:** Automatic renewal / continuous-service notice law enacted via **S2273 (2024)**, amending RI's unfair sales practices chapter (Title 6, Chapter 6-13 family). **Effective January 1, 2025.**
- **Pre-contract notice:** Businesses must present automatic-renewal / continuous-service contract terms clearly and conspicuously before the consumer enters the agreement.
- **Renewal notice:** Must give consumers notice prior to automatic renewal, including clear and conspicuous cancellation information, delivered in the same manner the consumer used to enter the contract.
- **Price-increase-specific timing (e.g., X days before renewal):** Not confirmed — the material located describes a renewal notice + cancellation-info requirement but does not specify an exact day count before renewal (contrast with SC's 30–60 day window). Verify exact timing requirement with counsel/current statute text.
- **Pending expansion:** 2026 bill (S2768) would require online-initiated auto-renewals to also be cancellable online — **not yet law**, monitor.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a standalone requirement distinct from the cancellation-method disclosure above.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Finding:** No Rhode Island-specific consumer EFT/ACH preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice statute distinct from federal Reg E (12 CFR 1005 / 15 U.S.C. § 1693 et seq.) was found. RI's money-transmission law (Title 19) references and requires compliance with federal EFTA/Reg E rather than layering its own preauthorized-debit notice regime.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether RI Gen. Laws Title 19, Chapter 14.3 (money transmission) or Title 6 imposes any additional preauthorized-debit disclosure beyond Reg E.
