# Tennessee — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially those flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107 ("Release of personal consumer information"), part of the Tennessee Identity Theft Deterrence Act.
- **Trigger:** "Breach of system security" = unauthorized acquisition of computerized data that materially compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal information maintained by an information holder. Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107(a).
- **Consumer notice deadline:** Not later than **45 days** from discovery or notification of the breach, unless law enforcement requires a longer delay (and if delayed for law enforcement, notice must follow within 45 days of the agency clearing it). Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107(b), (d).
- **State/CRA notification threshold:** If notice is required to **more than 1,000 persons at one time**, must also notify, without unreasonable delay, all nationwide consumer reporting agencies (as defined 15 U.S.C. § 1681a) of the timing, distribution, and content of the notices. Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107(g). (No separate AG-notice threshold identified in statute text reviewed — verify with counsel.)
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** **Yes.** "Personal information" = first name/initial + last name combined with account, credit card, or debit card number **in combination with any required security code, access code, or password permitting access to the individual's financial account.** Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107(a)(4).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — "encrypted" data (rendered unusable/unreadable/indecipherable per current FIPS 140-2) falls outside the notice trigger; safe harbor is undermined if the decryption key/process was also compromised (standard construction — confirm exact statutory carve-out language with counsel).
- **Penalties:** Violations constitute an unfair/deceptive trade practice under the Tennessee Consumer Protection Act; AG/reporter enforcement with a **60-day cure period**; civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**.
- **Private right of action:** **Yes.** A customer (person or business entity, excluding government) injured by a violation may bring a civil action for damages and injunctive relief. Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-2107(f).
- **GLBA/HIPAA exemption:** Entities subject to GLBA or HIPAA are exempt from this statute.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Statute:** Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-133 (Automatic renewal / continuous service offers, amended 2024 via HB1652/related legislation).
- **Pre-charge disclosure:** Auto-renewal/continuous-service terms must be presented **clearly and conspicuously** before the transaction is fulfilled, in visual or temporal proximity to the request for consent; if a free trial/gift is offered, the post-trial price must also be clearly disclosed.
- **Affirmative consent + retainable acknowledgment:** Business must obtain affirmative consent before charging the consumer's card/account and provide an acknowledgment (retainable by the consumer) stating the auto-renewal terms, cancellation policy, and how to cancel.
- **Advance renewal notice (delayed renewals):** If the renewal will occur **more than 60 days** after initial enrollment, the business must send the consumer a notice of the upcoming renewal/charge before billing. (Specific day-count for this notice **not confirmed** — verify current text of § 47-18-133.)
- **Material change notice:** Any material change to previously-accepted auto-renewal/continuous-service terms requires clear and conspicuous notice plus cancellation instructions, in a retainable format.
- **Online cancellation right:** Tennessee also requires that a service sold to consumers online be cancellable online without additional steps (2022 enactment, HB1652) — relevant to autopay/subscription cancellation-confirmation practices; exact cancellation-confirmation notice content **not confirmed**.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Not confirmed.** No Tennessee-specific statute identified imposing notice requirements for preauthorized EFT/recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005)/EFTA. Treat federal Reg E as the operative standard absent a state overlay.
