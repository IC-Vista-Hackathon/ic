# Georgia — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification — Georgia Personal Identity Protection Act

- **Statute:** O.C.G.A. § 10-1-912 (definitions in § 10-1-911).
- **Trigger:** Breach of the security of a system compromising unencrypted/unredacted "personal information" of a Georgia resident, discovered by an information broker/data collector (or by a third party maintaining data on their behalf).
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **No fixed statutory deadline** — notice required "in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," subject to legitimate law-enforcement needs and scope/remediation investigation. Third-party data handlers must notify the information broker/data collector within **24 hours** of discovery.
- **AG notification:** **Not required** by the general statute — no state-agency notification threshold.
- **CRA notice:** Required if notice goes to **more than 10,000 Georgia residents at one time** — notify nationwide CRAs without unreasonable delay (timing/distribution/content of notices).
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** Yes — "personal information" includes account number or credit/debit card number in combination with any required security code, access code, or password, or any of these alone if sufficient to permit identity theft.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — notice not required if the name or data elements were encrypted or redacted at the time of breach. Statute is silent on encryption-key compromise. **Not confirmed** how a compromised key is treated — verify with counsel.
- **Penalties:** **No specific statutory penalty** in § 10-1-912 itself and **no dedicated regulatory enforcement mechanism**. A violation may potentially be pursued as an unfair or deceptive practice under the Georgia Fair Business Practices Act (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.), which can carry penalties up to **$100 per violation per consumer** — **not confirmed** that this theory has been tested against the breach-notification statute specifically.
- **Private right of action:** **No** private right of action under the breach-notification statute itself. Note the general FBPA (§ 10-1-393 et seq.) does allow individual (non-class) private suits for FBPA violations broadly, but this is separate from — and not confirmed to extend to — § 10-1-912 breach-notice failures.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **Online Automatic Renewal Transparency Act:** O.C.G.A. § 10-1-439.9, effective **January 1, 2024** (replaced the prior general auto-renewal statute, O.C.G.A. § 13-12-3, for online offers).
  - Applies to businesses that let a consumer accept an automatic renewal or continuous-service offer **online**.
  - Requires notice **prior to, or within 3 days after,** charging the consumer's credit/debit card or third-party payment account for the renewal.
  - Notice must clearly and conspicuously state: (1) that the service auto-renews unless canceled; (2) the length/terms of the renewal period; (3) if electronic, a link or accessible method to the cancellation process.
  - Requires an online cancellation method (clear link, or a pre-formatted cancellation email the consumer can send without further explanation).
- **Legacy statute:** O.C.G.A. § 13-12-3 (notice to consumer prior to automatic renewal of a service contract) — still relevant for non-online contracts; **not confirmed** current scope post-2024 amendment, verify with counsel.
- **Price-increase notice (general, non-renewal):** No standalone Georgia statute located requiring advance notice of a price increase outside the auto-renewal notice window. **Not confirmed** — verify with counsel for sector-specific rules.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** No separate confirmation-notice requirement located beyond the cancellation-method mandate above.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Georgia-specific statute located that adds notice obligations for preauthorized/recurring EFT or ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 C.F.R. § 1005.10) / EFTA § 913 (15 U.S.C. § 1693e). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
