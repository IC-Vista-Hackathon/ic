# North Carolina — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify current text and any pending amendments with counsel before relying on this for a specific determination.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** N.C. Gen. Stat. § 75-65 (Identity Theft Protection Act, Ch. 75, Art. 2A, §§ 75-60 et seq.)

- **Trigger:** Any business that owns/licenses NC residents' "personal information" (computerized, paper, or other form) must notify affected persons following discovery of a "security breach" (unauthorized access to/acquisition of unencrypted/unredacted personal information causing or likely to cause harm).
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed number of days — "without unreasonable delay," consistent with law enforcement needs and the time needed to determine scope/restore system integrity.
- **State AG notice:** Required, "without unreasonable delay," for breaches of any size (§ 75-65(e1)); must include nature of breach, number of NC residents affected, steps taken, and prevention measures. No separate numeric threshold identified in the statute text reviewed.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** Not confirmed — the statute's PI definition (§ 75-61(10)) does not appear (per the sources reviewed) to explicitly enumerate "payment card number + security code" the way Ohio's does; it does include Social Security number, drivers license/state ID, financial account/card numbers in combination with required access codes, and other elements. Confirm exact enumerated list in § 75-61 with counsel.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** None identified in this section — unlike many states, NC's definition centers on "unauthorized access to or acquisition of" data, and no blanket encryption exemption was found in the text reviewed. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- **Penalties:** A violation is a violation of N.C. Gen. Stat. § 75-1.1 (unfair/deceptive trade practices) — no separate fixed civil penalty schedule found; § 75-1.1 remedies (including treble damages in private UDTP actions generally) may apply.
- **Private right of action:** Limited — "No private right of action may be brought by an individual for a violation of this section unless such individual is injured as a result of the violation." Causes of action may not be assigned.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statute:** N.C. Gen. Stat. § 75-41 (Contracts with automatic renewal clauses)

- Applies to any person in commerce selling/leasing goods or services under a contract that auto-renews unless canceled by the consumer.
- **Disclosure at contract formation:** Must disclose the automatic-renewal clause "clearly and conspicuously" in the contract/offer, and disclose clearly and conspicuously how to cancel (in the initial contract/offer or with delivery).
- **Pre-renewal notice (auto-renewals >60 days):** Written notice by personal delivery, email, or first-class mail, **at least 15 days but not more than 45 days** before the renewal date, stating the renewal date and that the contract will auto-renew unless canceled.
- **Changed terms:** If terms change upon renewal, the change must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously on the renewal notice in **at least 12-point bold type**.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no explicit statutory requirement for a cancellation-confirmation notice was found in § 75-41 text reviewed.
- **Price-increase notice (outside auto-renewal context):** Not confirmed — no general NC statute requiring advance notice of a price increase for ongoing (non-auto-renewing) recurring charges was found.
- **Safe harbor / cure:** No violation if the business shows compliance procedures are routine practice, the failure was an error, and it refunds/credits all amounts billed from the renewal date to termination (or the next renewal notice), whichever is first. Otherwise the auto-renewal clause is void and unenforceable.
- **Exemptions:** Insurers (Ch. 58), banks/trust companies/savings institutions/credit unions, and entities regulated by the FCC or NC Utilities Commission.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- No NC-specific consumer statute imposing EFT/ACH notice obligations beyond federal Reg E (12 C.F.R. Part 1005) was located. NC Gen. Stat. Ch. 25, Art. 4A adopts UCC Article 4A, but that governs *wholesale* (commercial) funds transfers between banks, not consumer preauthorized-transfer notices. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether any NC Banking Commission rule imposes additional consumer EFT notice duties.
