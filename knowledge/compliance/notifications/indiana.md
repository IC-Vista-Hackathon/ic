# Indiana — Notification Law Reference

> **Internal compliance reference only — not legal advice.** Verify all citations with counsel before relying on them for a specific incident or contract decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** Disclosure of Security Breach, Indiana Code 24-4.9-1 et seq.

- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of unencrypted personal information (or encrypted PI where the decryption key was also compromised) that "could result in identity deception, identity theft, or fraud." IC 24-4.9-3-1.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** Without unreasonable delay, **no more than 45 days after discovery** of the breach (tightened from a vague "unreasonable delay" standard, effective July 1, 2022). IC 24-4.9-3-1, IC 24-4.9-3-3.
  - Permitted delay reasons: restoring system integrity, determining breach scope, or a law-enforcement/AG request to delay because disclosure would impede an investigation.
- **State AG notice:** Required whenever consumer notice is given; **also within 45 days.**
- **Consumer reporting agencies:** Required if the breach affects **1,000 or more** consumers; must provide information needed to prevent fraud. IC 24-4.9-3-1.
- **Payment card number + security code as "personal information":** **Partially — nuance matters.** A credit card number alone is covered (no security code needed). A debit card number or financial account number is only covered **in combination with** a security code, password, or access code permitting account access. IC 24-4.9-2-10.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** **Yes.** Definition of "personal information" excludes encrypted or redacted data unless the breach also compromised the means to decrypt/unredact it. IC 24-4.9-2-10, IC 24-4.9-2-5.
- **Penalties:** Failure to comply is a "deceptive act," **actionable only by the Attorney General** — not a general civil penalty schedule specific to this chapter. IC 24-4.9-4-1. Related Deceptive Consumer Sales Act penalty tiers (context, not this chapter directly): incurable deceptive act fines up to $500/violation; knowing violations up to $5,000/violation. **Not confirmed which of these general DCSA penalty amounts a court would apply to an IC 24-4.9 violation — verify with counsel.**
- **Private right of action:** **No.** Statute expressly limits enforcement to the Attorney General. IC 24-4.9-4-1.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

**Statute:** Indiana Code 24-5-27 (automatic renewal of service/property contracts).

- Merchant providing maintenance, repair, or sale-of-real-property services, or maintenance/repair/lease/sale of personal property under a contract with an automatic renewal clause, must give **written notice at least 30 days before renewal**, including: (1) the renewal date, (2) a statement that the recipient must notify the merchant to terminate at term end, and (3) any promotions, discounts, additional fees, or fee increases. Effective July 1, 2010. Does not apply to leases/rentals of real property.
- **Price-increase notice, autopay/subscription renewal notice generally, cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed beyond the IC 24-5-27 renewal-notice content above — no broader Indiana statute located. Verify with counsel, particularly for contracts outside the maintenance/repair/lease/sale-of-property scope IC 24-5-27 covers.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- **Statute:** Indiana UCC Article 4.1 (Funds Transfers), IC 26-1-4.1, expressly **does not apply** to any transfer governed by the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act (15 U.S.C. 1693 et seq.) — i.e., Indiana defers to federal Reg E for consumer EFTs.
- No Indiana-specific consumer notice requirement beyond Reg E was identified. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
