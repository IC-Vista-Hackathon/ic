# Kentucky — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** KRS § 365.732 (general breach law); separate state-agency statute at KRS § 61.933.
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of unencrypted and unredacted computerized data that compromises security/confidentiality/integrity of PII, where the acquisition **actually causes or is reasonably believed to cause identity theft or fraud** against a KY resident (harm-threshold trigger, not mere acquisition).
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day-count. Standard is "most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," subject to law-enforcement delay and time needed to determine scope/restore system integrity.
- **AG notice:** No AG notification requirement found in KRS 365.732 itself.
- **Consumer reporting agency / CRA notice:** Required **without unreasonable delay** if breach affects **more than 1,000 persons at one time** — must notify all nationwide consumer reporting agencies/credit bureaus.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PII:** **Yes.** PII = first name/initial + last name plus "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password."
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — breach definition is limited to "unencrypted and unredacted" data; properly encrypted/redacted data falls outside the trigger.
- **Exemptions:** HIPAA-covered entities and GLBA-regulated financial institutions are exempt (deemed compliant via their own sectoral breach rules); state agencies/local government covered separately under KRS 61.933.
- **Penalties:** Not specified in KRS 365.732 (no statutory civil penalty schedule found).
- **Private right of action:** Not created by KRS 365.732 itself; commentary indicates an injured party may potentially pursue a claim under the general KY civil remedy statute, KRS 446.070. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Automatic renewal / continuous service statute:** KRS § 365.400 et seq. Requires **clear and conspicuous** disclosure, before the consumer accepts the offer, of: (a) that the subscription/purchase continues until cancelled, (b) the cancellation procedure, (c) the recurring charge amount, (d) that the charge amount may change and to what amount if known, and (e) the length of the renewal term.
- **Price-increase notice:** No standalone advance-notice-before-price-increase requirement identified separate from the disclosure above; KRS 365.400 requires disclosing *at signup* that the charge may change. **Not confirmed whether a separate pre-increase notice is mandated — verify with counsel.**
- **Renewal/autopay notice timing:** Not found as a distinct numbered-days requirement (contrast with Maine/Louisiana/Maryland, which specify day windows). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not explicitly mandated in the sections reviewed (KRS 365.400–365.408 cover the offer-disclosure and cancellation-mechanism requirements; no explicit "send confirmation of cancellation" clause located). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Kentucky-specific statute was found imposing notice requirements for preauthorized EFT/recurring debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** before concluding no gap-filling state requirement exists.
