# Oklahoma — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify current text and any pending amendments with counsel before relying on this for a specific determination.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** Security Breach Notification Act, 24 Okla. Stat. §§ 161–166, as amended by **SB 626** (2025), effective **January 1, 2026**

- **Trigger (§ 163):** Covered entity holding OK residents' computerized personal information must give notice "without unreasonable delay" after determining a breach occurred, subject to law-enforcement delay.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count in the base statute — "without unreasonable delay" following determination/notification of breach.
- **State AG notice (new under SB 626, eff. 1/1/2026):** Required for breaches affecting **500 or more** OK residents — must be sent "without unreasonable delay" and **no later than 60 days after** resident notice; must include breach date, determination date, nature of breach, PI types exposed, number of residents affected, estimated monetary impact, and safeguards in place.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Required when breach affects **more than 1,000 residents**.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** Base statute (pre-SB 626 text reviewed) refers generically to "unencrypted and unredacted personal information," without confirming an explicit payment-card-number-plus-security-code enumeration. SB 626 **expands** the PI definition to include biometric identifiers and additional electronic identifiers/credentials for financial-account access. **Exact enumerated list including payment card + CVV — not confirmed against full amended statutory text; verify with counsel.**
- **Encryption/redaction safe harbor:** Yes — notice not required if the accessed PI was encrypted or redacted, unless the unauthorized person also acquired the encryption key, or the PI was accessed/acquired in unencrypted/unredacted form.
- **Exemption thresholds:** Reported secondary-source thresholds of **fewer than 500 residents** (general AG-notice exemption) and **fewer than 1,000 residents at credit bureaus** — these predate/may be superseded by SB 626's 500-resident AG-notice trigger; **reconcile the two before relying on either; verify with counsel.**
- **Penalties (SB 626):** Civil penalties of up to **$150,000 per breach** (or series of related breaches from one investigation), sought by the OK AG or a district attorney; a graduated/reduced-penalty affirmative defense (down to **$75,000**) is available if the entity demonstrates it had reasonable security safeguards in place at the time of the breach.
- **Private right of action:** Not confirmed in the sources reviewed — the statute is generally understood as AG/DA-enforcement only; **verify with counsel.**

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statute:** Oklahoma Fair Renewal Act, codified at **15 Okla. Stat. §§ 773–773.6**, enacted via HB 1851 (2025) — passed House 3/26/2025, passed Senate 5/6/2025, **effective November 1, 2025**.

*(Note: search-engine and one bill-tracking snippet showed a conflicting "dead"/April 2027 status for HB 1851 — that appears to be a tracking-site error or confusion with a different bill; the Oklahoma Legislature's own bill-history data and multiple independent secondary sources agree the bill passed both chambers and takes effect 11/1/2025. Confirm current in-force text at oklegislature.gov before relying on exact provisions.)*

- **Core duty:** A seller of goods/services under an "automatic renewal contract" must notify the consumer that the contract will automatically renew/continue unless canceled, and must explain the cancellation process; the cancellation process must give clear, accurate sender-identity information.
- **Reminder notice window:** Reminder notice must be sent **between 15 and 45 days before** the contract renews, detailing how to cancel.
- **Material term changes:** Business must notify consumers of "any significant changes" to the automatic-renewal contract terms (exact notice-timing/format requirements for term changes — **not confirmed against full statutory text; verify with counsel**).
- **Free trial offers:** Any free-trial offer must clearly disclose price and further obligations that begin once the trial ends.
- **Cancellation mechanism:** Must provide an easy, accessible cancellation method (e.g., a direct online cancellation link, or in-person if the service is regularly used in person).
- **Written acknowledgment:** Consumer must receive written acknowledgment of the offer terms and cancellation policy that they can retain.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Referenced in secondary summaries but exact statutory text/format requirement **not confirmed; verify with counsel.**
- **Price-increase notice (outside auto-renewal context):** Not confirmed — no general OK statute requiring advance notice of price increases on non-auto-renewing recurring charges was found.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- No Oklahoma-specific consumer statute imposing EFT/ACH notice obligations beyond federal Reg E (12 C.F.R. Part 1005) was located. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
