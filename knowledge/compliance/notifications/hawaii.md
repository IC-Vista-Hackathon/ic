# Hawaii — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification — Security Breach of Personal Information Act

- **Statute:** Haw. Rev. Stat. Chapter 487N (definitions § 487N-1; notice § 487N-2).
- **Trigger:** Security breach compromising unencrypted "personal information" of a Hawaii resident, held by a business or government agency.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **No fixed number of days** — notice required "without unreasonable delay," subject to legitimate law-enforcement needs and the time needed to determine scope/restore system integrity.
- **State notification:** Required if notice goes to **more than 1,000 persons at one time** — must notify, in writing and without unreasonable delay, the State Office of Consumer Protection.
- **CRA notice:** Same 1,000+ trigger — notify nationwide consumer reporting agencies without unreasonable delay.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** Yes — "personal information" includes an account number, credit or debit card number, or an access code/password that would permit access to an individual's financial account, combined with name.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — breached data that was encrypted or redacted does not trigger notice, provided the confidential process/key was not also compromised.
- **Penalties:** Civil penalty of **not more than $2,500 per violation**; enforceable by the Attorney General or the Executive Director of the Office of Consumer Protection.
- **Private right of action:** **Yes** — Chapter 487N permits a private cause of action. **Not confirmed** as to specific damages/remedy scope — verify with counsel.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **Automatic renewal / continuous service law:** Haw. Rev. Stat. § 481-9.5.
  - Applies to a consumer contract with an "automatic renewal clause" — a plan/arrangement where a paid subscription or purchasing agreement with a term of **more than 1 month** automatically renews for a further term of **more than 1 month**.
  - Automatic-renewal clause and cancellation procedure must be disclosed **clearly and conspicuously** in the contract.
  - Renewal notice must be sent **no less than 30 days and no more than 60 days** before the date the consumer must act to prevent renewal.
  - Electronic notice permitted if the transaction was conducted electronically or the consumer elected electronic communications.
  - Knowing violation, or failure to honor a cancellation request, is an unfair method of competition / unfair or deceptive trade practice.
- **Price-increase notice (general, non-renewal):** No standalone Hawaii statute located requiring advance notice of a price increase outside the § 481-9.5 renewal-notice window. **Not confirmed** — verify with counsel for sector-specific rules.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** No separate confirmation-notice requirement located beyond the cancellation-procedure disclosure above.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Hawaii-specific statute located that adds notice obligations for preauthorized/recurring EFT or ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 C.F.R. § 1005.10) / EFTA § 913 (15 U.S.C. § 1693e). **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
