# Oregon — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Oregon Consumer Information Protection Act (OCIPA), ORS 646A.600–646A.628 (notice provisions at **ORS 646A.604**; definitions at **ORS 646A.602**).
- **Trigger:** "Breach of security" = unauthorized acquisition of computerized data that materially compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal information.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** As soon as practicable, **no later than 45 days** after discovering or being notified of the breach.
- **AG / CRA notice:** If breach affects **more than 250 Oregon consumers**, must send the Oregon DOJ a copy of the notice and a report, also within 45 days.
- **"Personal information" includes payment card + code?** Yes — explicitly covers "a consumer's financial account number, credit card number or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code or password" (ORS 646A.602).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. Data elements are not "personal information" if encryption/redaction/other methods have not been rendered unusable, OR if encrypted and the encryption key was not also acquired — i.e., encrypted data with the key intact falls outside scope.
- **Penalties:** Enforced by Oregon DOJ under Unlawful Trade Practices Act; civil penalty up to **$1,000 per violation, capped at $500,000** for a continuing violation (ORS 646A.624).
- **Private right of action:** **No** — enforcement is AG-only under OCIPA. (Note: Oregon also has a separate, newer Oregon Consumer Privacy Act, ORS 646A.570–589, dealing with general data privacy, not breach notice — also AG-enforced only, penalties up to $7,500/violation.)

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **Statute:** Automatic renewal / continuous service law, **ORS 646A.293 (definitions) and 646A.295 (requirements)**.
- **Pre-charge notice:** Before charging a consumer's card/payment account for an automatic renewal or continuous service, the business must present the auto-renewal/continuous-service terms clearly and conspicuously, in visual (or temporal, if by voice) proximity to the consent request, and obtain affirmative consent.
- **Confirmation notice:** Must provide an acknowledgment of the auto-renewal/continuous-service terms and cancellation instructions, in a form the consumer can retain (e.g., email).
- **Material change notice:** If auto-renewal/continuous-service terms materially change after a consumer has accepted, must give clear and conspicuous notice of the change plus cancellation instructions, retainable by the consumer.
- **Price-increase-specific notice / renewal-timing notice (e.g., X days before renewal):** Not confirmed — the Oregon statute located does not specify a fixed number of days before renewal for a reminder notice (unlike SC); it is a consent/acknowledgment/material-change model, not a pre-renewal-reminder model. Verify with counsel if a specific "days before renewal" notice is needed.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a distinct standalone requirement beyond the cancellation-method disclosure above.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Finding:** No Oregon-specific consumer EFT/ACH preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice statute distinct from federal Reg E (12 CFR 1005) was found. Oregon's own "electronic funds transfer" statutes located (ORS 316.198, OAR chapters) govern state tax payment methods, not consumer preauthorized debits.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether any Oregon banking-code provision separately layers onto Reg E for preauthorized consumer debits.
