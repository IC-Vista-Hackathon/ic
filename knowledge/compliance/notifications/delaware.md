# Delaware — Notification Law Reference

**Not legal advice.** Internal compliance reference only; verify with counsel before relying on any item below.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** 6 Del. C. Chapter 12B, § 12B-102.

- **Trigger:** Breach of security of computerized data that includes personal information of a DE resident, unless the business reasonably determines (after appropriate investigation) that the breach is unlikely to result in harm.
- **Deadline to consumers:** Without unreasonable delay, **not later than 60 days** after determination of the breach, unless shorter federal deadline applies. If a subset of affected residents can't be identified within 60 days despite reasonable diligence, notice to them is due "as soon as practicable" thereafter (unless substitute notice was already given).
- **AG notice:** Required if affected DE residents **exceed 500**, **no later than the time** notice is given to residents.
- **Consumer reporting agencies:** No statutory requirement to notify CRAs found in Chapter 12B.
- **Payment card + security code covered?** **Yes** — "account number, credit card number, or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" is expressly listed PI.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — unauthorized acquisition of encrypted data isn't a reportable breach unless the encryption key was also acquired and there's a reasonable belief it could render the data readable/usable.
- **Credit monitoring:** Distinctive DE requirement — if an SSN is breached, must offer **1 year** of free credit monitoring.
- **Penalties:** AG enforcement action; specific civil penalty amount **not confirmed in primary text — verify with counsel.**
- **Private right of action:** **No** — no private right of action identified in Chapter 12B.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statute:** 6 Del. C. § 2734 (Contracts with automatic renewal provisions), Title 6 Ch. 27, Subchapter IV.

- **Disclosure at signup:** Automatic renewal terms must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously at the time the contract is entered into.
- **Autopay/subscription renewal notice:** Applies where a contract renews for a period of **more than 1 month** and the renewal extends the contract **beyond 12 months** from initiation — seller must notify the consumer of each upcoming extension **no less than 30 and no more than 60 days** before the cancellation deadline, disclosing (1) that it will auto-renew absent cancellation, (2) the cancellation deadline date, and (3) the cancellation procedure.
- **Cancellation mechanism:** If the contract was entered online, seller must provide an online cancellation procedure.
- **Price increase notice / cancellation confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as distinct statutory requirements beyond the renewal-notice regime above — **verify with counsel.**
- **Remedies:** Violation makes the seller liable to the consumer for **treble actual damages plus reasonable attorneys' fees** — this is a private remedy, notable contrast to the breach-notice statute's AG-only enforcement.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Delaware-specific statute imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice requirements beyond federal Reg E was identified in this research. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel**, particularly under Title 5 (Banking) for any state-chartered-institution overlay.
