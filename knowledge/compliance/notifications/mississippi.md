# Mississippi — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification — Miss. Code § 75-24-29

- **Statute cite:** Miss. Code Ann. § 75-24-29.
- **Trigger:** Applies to any person who conducts business in Mississippi and who, in the ordinary course of business, owns, licenses, or maintains personal information of an MS resident. Duty arises on "breach of security" = unauthorized acquisition of electronic files/media/databases/computerized data containing personal information, where access has not been secured by encryption or another method rendering it unreadable/unusable.
- **Deadline to notify consumers:** No fixed day count — **"without unreasonable delay,"** subject to completing an investigation to determine the nature/scope of the incident and identify affected individuals, and subject to law-enforcement delay requests (delay permitted while a law enforcement agency determines notice would impede a criminal investigation or national security; notice resumes once LE says it's clear).
- **AG notice threshold/deadline:** **No mandatory AG-notice provision found** in the statute text reviewed — the AG's role is enforcement only (see Penalties), not a required recipient of pre-notice. This is a difference from states like MN with hard CRA deadlines. Not confirmed beyond what's in the statute — verify with counsel in case guidance/regulations impose one.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** No CRA-specific threshold or deadline (e.g., no MN-style 500-person/48-hour rule) was found in § 75-24-29. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** **Yes, explicitly.** Covered data = first name/initial + last name combined with SSN, driver's license/state ID, or "account number or credit or debit card number in combination with any required security code, access code or password."
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. Data protected by encryption or another method/technology rendering personal information unreadable/unusable is excluded from the breach trigger.
- **Penalties:** Failure to comply constitutes an **unfair trade practice**, enforced by the Attorney General.
- **Private right of action:** **Explicitly prohibited.** Statute states nothing in the section "may be construed to create a private right of action."

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices (auto-renewal, price increase, cancellation confirmation)

- **Status: No enacted, general Mississippi statute found.** SB 2498 (2025 session) would have required a pre-renewal notice **between 7 and 21 days** before the automatic renewal date (for subscriptions >1 month), full-refund remedies for charging before required acknowledgment/notice, and a 10-day post-renewal cancellation refund right. **Per legislative tracking reviewed, SB 2498 passed the Senate but stalled in the House Business and Commerce Committee — it was not enacted into law** despite an "effective July 1, 2025" clause in the introduced/engrossed text; treat that effective date as contingent on passage, not operative. A companion bill, HB 875 (2025), addressing electronic auto-renewal disclosure proximity/placement, was also introduced but not confirmed as enacted.
- **Action item:** Re-check Mississippi Legislature bill-status pages closer to the 2026 session in case either bill (or a successor) was revived and passed — status as of this research (mid-2026) is "not enacted," but verify current session activity before relying on this.
- No Mississippi-specific price-increase-notice or cancellation-confirmation statute was identified. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- No Mississippi-specific statute imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice requirements beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR 1005.10) was located. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether Mississippi's general consumer-protection/unfair-trade-practices statute (Miss. Code § 75-24-1 et seq., the same chapter as the breach law) has been used by the AG to reach recurring-debit notice practices in enforcement actions, even absent a dedicated EFT statute.
