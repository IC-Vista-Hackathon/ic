# Missouri — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice — verify with counsel before relying on this for a live incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Mo. Rev. Stat. § 407.1500
- **Trigger:** "Breach of security" = unauthorized access to *and* unauthorized acquisition of computerized personal information that compromises security, confidentiality, or integrity of the data.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — "without unreasonable delay" following discovery/notification of the breach.
- **AG / consumer reporting agency notice:** Required "without unreasonable delay" when notice is being given to more than 1,000 Missouri consumers at one time; notify nationwide consumer reporting agencies of timing/distribution/content. No separate AG-notice trigger below that threshold is specified in the statute.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** Yes — "financial account number, credit card number, or debit card number in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" is explicitly personal information.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — data that is "encrypted, redacted, or otherwise altered ... such that the name or data elements are unreadable or unusable" falls outside the definition of personal information.
- **Penalties:** AG may seek civil penalty up to $150,000 per breach (or series of related breaches).
- **Private right of action:** No — enforcement is AG-exclusive.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Price increase / recurring charge / autopay renewal notice:** Not confirmed — general web search found no Missouri-specific automatic-renewal statute analogous to CA/other ARL states. Missouri's Merchandising Practices Act (Ch. 407) does not appear to impose a generic pre-charge or price-increase notice requirement for subscriptions.
- **Health spa / club membership cancellation:**
  - Mo. Rev. Stat. § 407.330 — health spa contracts must allow penalty-free cancellation within 3 business days of signing, written notice (certified mail or personal delivery), refund within 30 days of receipt.
  - Mo. Rev. Stat. § 407.672 — club memberships generally cancellable by written notice before midnight of the 3rd business day after joining; refund of full consideration; mailed notice effective on deposit.
  - These are point-of-sale cooling-off rights, not recurring-charge/renewal notice requirements — do not treat as a general autopay notice rule.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no statute found requiring an affirmative cancellation-confirmation notice outside the health spa/club context above.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Missouri statute found imposing EFT/ACH notice requirements beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 205, incl. § 1005.10 preauthorized transfers). Treat federal Reg E as the floor and ceiling absent further findings.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** if a Missouri-specific banking regulation applies to your product's transfer mechanism.
