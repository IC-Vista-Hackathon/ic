# Nevada — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice — verify with counsel before relying on this for a live incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** NRS Chapter 603A (Security and Privacy of Personal Information); notice duty at NRS 603A.220, safe harbor at 603A.215, penalties at 603A.360.
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of unencrypted personal information that materially compromises its security, confidentiality, or integrity.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — "most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," consistent with law-enforcement needs and scope-determination measures. (Do not rely on informal "30–45 day" guidance sometimes cited by third parties — the statute itself sets no numeric deadline.)
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** If notice must go to more than 1,000 persons at one time, must also notify nationwide consumer reporting agencies, without unreasonable delay, of the timing/content of the notice.
- **AG notice:** Not confirmed as a standalone mandatory-notice trigger in NRS 603A.220 itself — enforcement authority sits with the Nevada AG (603A.290) but verify with counsel whether a proactive AG-notice duty applies to your fact pattern.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** Yes — name combined with "account number, credit card number or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code or password" (NRS 603A.040).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — a data collector is not liable for a breach if it was in compliance with NRS 603A's encryption/security requirements and the breach was not caused by the collector's gross negligence or intentional misconduct (NRS 603A.215).
- **Penalties:** Civil penalty up to $5,000 per violation for operators/data brokers under related deceptive-trade-practice provisions (NRS 603A.360, tied to NRS 598.0903 et seq.).
- **Private right of action:** No — NRS 603A.300–603A.360 expressly do not create a private right of action against an operator.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **General autopay/subscription renewal notice statute:** Not confirmed as a broad consumer statute — Nevada's automatic-renewal protections are narrowly scoped to dance studio / health club contracts (NRS 598.9415–598.950): such contracts may **not** contain an automatic-renewal clause at all; buyer may cancel within 3 business days of receiving the contract (written notice, in person or postmarked by midnight of day 3); refund of all money paid within 15 days of the studio/club receiving the cancellation notice.
- **Price-increase notice / recurring-charge notice (general commerce):** Not confirmed — no Nevada statute found requiring general advance notice of a price increase or recurring charge outside the dance-studio/health-club context.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice requirement:** Not confirmed generally; the dance-studio/health-club statute effectively requires a refund action (not a separate confirmation notice) within 15 days of cancellation.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Nevada-specific consumer EFT/ACH notice statute found beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 205, incl. § 1005.10 preauthorized transfers). NRS 660.105 addresses "electronic transfer of money" only as a construction/savings-clause provision for state-chartered institutions, not a consumer notice mandate.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** for Nevada Financial Institutions Division guidance beyond Reg E.
