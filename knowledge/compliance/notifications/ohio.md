# Ohio — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify current text and any pending amendments with counsel before relying on this for a specific determination.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** Ohio Rev. Code § 1349.19 (Private Disclosure of Security Breach of Computerized Personal Information Data), with related AG-investigation authority in §§ 1349.191–1349.192

- **Trigger:** Any person owning/licensing computerized data including "personal information" must disclose a breach to any Ohio resident whose PI was, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed and acquired by an unauthorized person, where that access causes or is reasonably believed will cause a material risk of identity theft or other fraud.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** "Most expedient time possible but not later than **forty-five (45) days**" following discovery or notification of the breach.
- **State AG notice:** No separate statutory deadline/threshold found in § 1349.19 itself; AG has investigative authority over compliance failures under §§ 1349.191–.192.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Required, "without unreasonable delay," when the breach requires disclosure to **more than 1,000 Ohio residents** — must cover timing, distribution, and content of the resident disclosures. This obligation cannot be used to delay the 45-day resident-notice deadline.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** Yes, explicitly — PI includes "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with and linked to any required security code, access code, or password" (alongside name + SSN, driver's license/state ID number).
- **Encryption/redaction safe harbor:** Yes — data elements that are "encrypted, redacted, or altered by any method or technology such that the data elements are unreadable" fall outside the PI definition entirely.
- **Penalties:** No specific civil-penalty schedule found in § 1349.19; enforcement runs through AG action under related sections. **Not confirmed — verify exact enforcement mechanics/remedies with counsel.**
- **Private right of action:** None under this section — enforcement authority rests solely with the Ohio Attorney General.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **No general Ohio automatic-renewal/subscription statute** comparable to NC/ND was identified. Ohio does **not** currently have a comprehensive auto-renewal law on the books.
- Ohio's Consumer Sales Practices Act (Ohio Rev. Code § 1345.01 et seq., incl. § 1345.43) provides general deceptive-practices coverage and may be read (via AG rules/enforcement) to require a reasonable cancellation mechanism, but this is a general framework, not a specific pre-renewal/price-increase notice mandate. **Not confirmed as a direct analog to NC §75-41/ND §51-37-02 — verify with counsel.**
- Sector-specific renewal-notice rules exist (e.g., competitive retail electric service contracts under OAC 4901:1-21-11, requiring 45–90 day advance renewal notice; prepaid entertainment contracts under OAC 4901:1-29-10, with a 3-7 business day cancellation window) but these are **utility/entertainment-contract specific**, not general payment/billing law, and are unlikely to apply to a payments company's own consumer billing directly.
- **Price-increase notice / cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no general statute found requiring these for ordinary recurring consumer charges.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- No Ohio-specific consumer statute imposing EFT/ACH notice obligations beyond federal Reg E (12 C.F.R. Part 1005) was located. **Not confirmed — verify with counsel.**
