# Massachusetts — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification — M.G.L. c. 93H

- **Statute cite:** Mass. Gen. Laws ch. 93H, §§ 1, 3 (definitions and duty to report); implementing regs at 201 CMR 17.00.
- **Trigger:** Person/agency "knows or has reason to know of a breach of security" OR knows/has reason to know personal information of a MA resident was acquired/used by an unauthorized person or for an unauthorized purpose. (§3)
- **Deadline to notify consumers:** No fixed day count — **"as soon as practicable and without unreasonable delay."** Confirmed current text; MA is one of the states using this standard rather than a numeric deadline.
- **AG / regulator notice:** Same "as soon as practicable and without unreasonable delay" standard. Notice goes to the Attorney General and the Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation (OCABR); no numeric-resident threshold found in the statute text reviewed — notice appears required regardless of breach size. Required AG/OCABR notice content: nature of the breach, number of MA residents affected, and steps taken/planned. (§3)
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Not established as a separate requirement in c. 93H itself (no 48-hour/other CRA rule found, unlike MN). Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
- **Payment card + security code as "personal information":** **Yes, explicitly.** Definition (§1) includes resident name + "financial account number, or credit or debit card number, with or without any required security code, access code, personal identification number or password."
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. "Breach of security" is defined around "unauthorized acquisition or unauthorized use of unencrypted data or encrypted electronic data and the confidential process or key" — i.e., encrypted data without the key generally falls outside the trigger. "Encrypted" = transformation via 128-bit or higher algorithmic process producing low probability of assigning meaning without the key; AG's office (Consumer Affairs) may update this definition by regulation.
- **Good-faith employee access exception:** Not a "breach" if a good-faith, authorized-purpose employee acquisition, unless info is later misused or improperly disclosed.
- **Penalties:** Not specified within c. 93H §§1/3 text reviewed; enforcement generally sits with the AG under c. 93A (unfair/deceptive practices). Not confirmed — verify exact penalty provision with counsel.
- **Private right of action:** Not confirmed in the sections reviewed — verify with counsel (c. 93A private-action mechanics may apply indirectly).

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices — 940 CMR 38.00 (AG "Unfair and Deceptive Fees" regs, incl. auto-renewal)

- **Cite:** 940 CMR 38.00, specifically § 38.05 (Recurring Fees and Trial Offers).
- **Renewal reminder window (Negative Option Feature > 31 days):** Written notice required **no more than 30 and no fewer than 5 calendar days** before the date the consumer must cancel to avoid a subsequent financial obligation.
  - Must clearly and conspicuously disclose: (a) any financial obligation incurred if consumer fails to cancel; (b) all products for which an obligation may be incurred; (c) the cancellation mechanism.
- **Negative Option Feature ≤ 31 days:** Notice must be given through a medium substantially similar to the one the consumer used to initiate the feature, or another commonly-used medium reasonably calculated to be seen/understood by an ordinary consumer.
- **Price-increase-specific notice:** Not identified as a distinct requirement separate from the renewal-notice regime above. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not directly located in the sources reviewed for 38.05; the regulation's cancellation-mechanism requirements imply confirmation practices but exact text not confirmed. Verify with counsel.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- **Cite:** M.G.L. c. 167B, § 8; implementing regs 209 CMR 31.00 (Electronic Fund Transfers).
- **Change-in-terms notice:** A financial institution must notify a consumer **in writing at least 30 days prior** to the effective date of any change in a required-to-be-disclosed account term/condition, if the change increases cost/liability or decreases account access. Notice must explain the significance of the change and the consumer's right to terminate if they don't accept it.
  - Exception: a financial institution may implement a change without prior notice if necessary to maintain/restore the security of an EFT system or account.
- **Preauthorized transfer notice:** 209 CMR 31.10 states compliance with 12 CFR 1005.10 (Reg E) constitutes compliance with the state rule — i.e., Massachusetts largely incorporates Reg E by reference for preauthorized-transfer notices rather than layering additional state-specific requirements.
