# South Carolina — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on this for a specific incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** **S.C. Code Ann. § 39-1-90** (2009).
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized access to and acquisition of computerized "personal identifying information" (not rendered unusable via encryption/redaction) where illegal use has occurred or is reasonably likely, or where use creates a material risk of harm to a resident.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** "Most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay" — **no fixed day count** in the statute.
- **AG / CRA notice:** If **more than 1,000** SC residents are notified, must notify the Consumer Protection Division of the SC Dept. of Consumer Affairs, and all nationwide consumer reporting agencies, without unreasonable delay, as to timing/distribution/content.
- **"Personal information" includes payment card + code?** Yes — "financial account number, or credit card or debit card number in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" (card number alone does not qualify).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — data rendered "unusable" through encryption, redaction, or other methods is excluded from the definition of personal identifying information.
- **Penalties:** Administrative fine of **$1,000 per SC resident** whose information was accessible due to a knowing and willful violation.
- **Private right of action:** **Yes.** Under subsection (G): willful violations allow recovery of **all damages**; negligent violations allow recovery of **actual damages** only; injunctive relief and attorney's fees/costs also available to a prevailing plaintiff. This is the only one of the four states reviewed with a private right of action for breach notice violations.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment / Billing Notices

- **Statutes:** **S.C. Code § 38-78-55** (service contracts under Title 38 insurance code) and **§ 37-6-120** (Consumer Protection Code), both added/amended by **S.434, effective May 20, 2024**.
- **Renewal notice timing:** No automatic-renewal provision in a "service contract" is enforceable against the contract holder unless the holder was given written/electronic notice of the auto-renewal provision **not less than 30 days nor more than 60 days before the cancellation deadline**.
- **Required notice content:** Must conspicuously disclose (1) that the contract will auto-renew unless canceled, (2) the amount to be charged on renewal, and (3) cancellation methods, which must include a toll-free number, email address, mailing address (if directly billed), or another easy-to-use cancellation mechanism.
- **Scope limit:** § 37-6-120's "service contract" definition applies to contracts for natural persons/individuals for personal, family, or household purposes; excludes contracts for services from entities incorporated under Title 33, Chapter 49. Confirm whether a payments-company product (e.g., a billing/invoicing subscription for consumers) falls within this definition before relying on it — **not confirmed for every product type; verify with counsel.**
- **Price-increase-specific notice:** The renewal-amount disclosure above functions as the closest analog — no separate stand-alone "price increase" notice statute identified.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed as a distinct standalone requirement beyond the cancellation-method disclosure above.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Finding:** No South Carolina-specific consumer EFT/ACH preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice statute distinct from federal Reg E (12 CFR 1005) was located. Title 34 searches surfaced only unrelated provisions (institutional funds management, check-dishonor notices under § 34-11-60).
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether SC Consumer Protection Code (Title 37) layers any additional preauthorized-debit disclosure duties onto Reg E for SC consumers.
