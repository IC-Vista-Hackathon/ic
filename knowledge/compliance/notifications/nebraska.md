# Nebraska — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice — verify with counsel before relying on this for a live incident or product decision.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** Financial Data Protection and Consumer Notification of Data Security Breach Act of 2006, Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 87-801 to 87-808 (definitions at § 87-802, notice duty at § 87-803, AG remedy at § 87-806).
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of unencrypted computerized data that compromises security, confidentiality, or integrity of personal information; notice required if investigation shows unauthorized use has occurred or is reasonably likely to occur.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — "as soon as possible and without unreasonable delay" (§ 87-803(1)).
- **AG notice:** Required no later than the time consumer notice is given (§ 87-803(2)) — earlier/stricter than most states' 1,000-person thresholds; applies regardless of breach size.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Not confirmed — no separate CRA-notice deadline/threshold found in this Act.
- **Payment card + security code covered as PI:** Yes — "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" (§ 87-802(5)(a)(iii)).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — encrypted data is excluded from "personal information" unless the encryption key was also compromised (§ 87-802(1), (3)).
- **Penalties:** AG may seek and recover direct economic damages for each affected Nebraska resident (§ 87-806(1)); violations also treated as Consumer Protection Act violations (§ 87-806(2)).
- **Private right of action:** No — AG enforcement only.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Price increase / recurring charge / autopay renewal notice statute:** Not confirmed — general search found no Nebraska-specific automatic-renewal law. The Uniform Deceptive Trade Practices Act (Neb. Rev. Stat. § 87-302 et seq.) and Consumer Protection Act (§ 59-1524 et seq.) give the AG general authority over unfair/deceptive acts, which counsel has noted can reach auto-renewal issues in the absence of a specific statute — but this is a general enforcement backstop, not a defined notice-timing/content rule.
- **Health club / membership cancellation notice:** Not confirmed — no Nebraska-specific statute located comparable to Missouri's health-spa/club-cancellation provisions.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no statute found.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements

- No Nebraska-specific consumer EFT/ACH notice statute found beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 205, incl. § 1005.10 preauthorized transfers). (Nebraska DOR's EFT rules concern state tax remittance, not consumer notice — not applicable here.)
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** for any Nebraska Department of Banking and Finance guidance beyond Reg E.
