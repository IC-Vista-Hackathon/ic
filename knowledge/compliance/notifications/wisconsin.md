# Wisconsin — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Wis. Stat. § 134.98 ("Notice of unauthorized acquisition of personal information")
- **Trigger:** Entity whose principal place of business is in WI, or that maintains/licenses personal information of WI residents, **knows** that personal information in its possession was acquired by an unauthorized person. § 134.98(2)(a)
- **Consumer notice deadline:** Within a **reasonable time, not to exceed 45 days** after the entity learns of the acquisition. § 134.98(3)(a)
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Required, "without unreasonable delay," if breach affects **1,000+ individuals**. § 134.98(2)(br)
- **AG notice:** No statutory requirement to notify the Wisconsin AG or DATCP.
- **Payment card + security code covered as "personal information"?** **Yes** — name combined with financial account number (incl. credit/debit card number) or any security code/access code/password permitting access to the account. § 134.98(1)(b)3.
- **Encryption/redaction safe harbor:** **Yes** — data that is encrypted, redacted, or otherwise rendered unreadable is excluded from the definition of "personal information," so no duty is triggered. § 134.98(1)(b).
- **Risk-of-harm exception:** No notice required if the acquisition does not create a material risk of identity theft or fraud.
- **Penalties:** No express statutory penalty provision; enforced by the WI Attorney General.
- **Private right of action:** **No.** Case law confirms no private right of action exists under § 134.98 (*Fox v. Iowa Health System*, 399 F. Supp. 3d 780 (W.D. Wis. 2019)).

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **No general statute requiring notice before a price increase** — Not confirmed to exist; none found in WCA or DATCP rules for general pricing.
- **Automatic renewal / negative option (consumer contracts):** Wis. Admin. Code **ATCP 127** (adopted under Wis. Stat. § 100.20(2)) and the Negative Option statute, **Wis. Stat. § 100.195**.
  - No fixed-term subscription may auto-renew unless the consumer can cancel at any time, **or** the provider gives written notice reminding the consumer of the scheduled renewal **at least 30 days but not more than 60 days** before the renewal/extension date.
  - Notice must state the contract will renew/extend unless declined, and describe how/when to decline.
  - Negative-option billing: no charge for services the consumer did not affirmatively order; failure to reject an offer is not an affirmative order.
  - **Enforcement:** ATCP 127 violations prosecutable under § 100.20(6) and § 100.26(3)/(6). **Private right of action exists** under § 100.20(5): consumer harmed may sue for **2x actual loss** plus costs and attorney's fees.
- **Separate B2B automatic renewal statute:** Wis. Stat. **§ 134.49** covers business equipment leases / business services contracts (not typically consumer-facing). Initial term >1 year, auto-renewal >1 year: written notice required **at least 15 days, not more than 60 days** before the decline deadline; must disclose renewal term, any charge increase, and how/deadline to decline. Non-compliant auto-renewal clause becomes unenforceable; prevailing customer may recover **2x damages** or 2x the periodic payment or $1,000, whichever is less.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no standalone WI statute found mandating a specific cancellation-confirmation notice outside the ATCP 127 decline mechanics above.

## 3. EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Federal Reg E

- **Not confirmed** — no Wisconsin-specific statute found imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice requirements for recurring consumer debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). Wisconsin UCC Article 4A (Wis. Stat. ch. 410, subch. II) governs wholesale/commercial funds transfers and expressly does not apply where the federal EFTA governs the transfer — it is not a consumer recurring-debit notice regime.
- Verify with counsel whether any DFI-issued guidance imposes additional requirements on WCA-regulated creditors for recurring ACH debits tied to credit transactions.
