# Arizona — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** A.R.S. §§ 18-551 (definitions) and 18-552 (notification).
- **Trigger:** A "security incident" — unauthorized acquisition of and access to unencrypted/unredacted computerized personal information that materially compromises security or confidentiality, giving rise to a "security system breach."
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **Within 45 days** after determination that a security system breach occurred. §18-552(B).
- **AG notice:** Required if breach affects **more than 1,000** individuals — notify the Arizona Attorney General **and** the Director of the Arizona Department of Homeland Security, in writing, within the same **45 days**. Must also notify the three largest nationwide consumer reporting agencies. §18-552.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Same >1,000-individual trigger as AG notice (see above).
- **Payment card + security code as PII:** **Yes.** "Personal information" includes an individual's financial account number or credit/debit card number **in combination with** any required security code, access code, or password that would permit access to the account. §18-551.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** The statute applies only to "unencrypted and unredacted" computerized personal information — encrypted data falls outside the definition of a reportable security incident. No separate express "safe harbor" clause beyond this scoping language; treat as a de facto safe harbor. §18-551/.552.
- **Penalties:** AG may impose a civil penalty **not to exceed the lesser of $10,000 per affected individual or the total economic loss** to affected individuals; **capped at $500,000** per breach or series of related breaches. §18-552.
- **Private right of action:** **No.** Enforcement is by the Attorney General only.
- **Reasonable-investigation exemption:** No notification required if the entity, an independent third-party forensic auditor, or law enforcement determines after reasonable investigation the breach has not resulted in and is not reasonably likely to result in substantial economic loss to affected individuals.

## 2. Consumer Payment/Billing Notices

- **Auto-renewal / recurring charge / subscription-renewal notice law:** **No general Arizona auto-renewal statute currently in force.** HB 2951 (2026 session), which would have created a comprehensive "Subscription Regulations" article (A.R.S. Title 44, Ch. 9) requiring same-method cancellation and renewal notices **25–40 days** before each renewal, passed the House but **died** as of 2026-06-14 — it is **not law**. Do not rely on it. Not confirmed — re-check for reintroduction in a later session.
  - Arizona does have a **sector-specific** auto-renewal/cancellation-notice law for **health spa/health club contracts**: A.R.S. § 44-1793 requires conspicuous 3-day rescission notice and defined cancellation/suspension methods disclosed in the contract, with refunds due within 30 days of cancellation notice. This does not extend to general subscription or payment services.
- **Price-increase notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no general Arizona statute located requiring advance notice of a price increase for recurring consumer services.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no general statutory requirement located outside the health-spa sector law above.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Arizona consumer-facing statute was found imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice obligations beyond federal Regulation E. Not confirmed — verify with counsel.
