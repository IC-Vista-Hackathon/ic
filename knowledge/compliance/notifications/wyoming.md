# Wyoming — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Wyo. Stat. §§ 40-12-501 to 40-12-502 (definitions and breach notification duty), within the Wyoming Consumer Protection Act, Title 40, Ch. 12.
- **Trigger:** Individual/commercial entity conducting business in WY that owns or licenses computerized data with personal identifying information becomes aware of a "breach of the security of the data system" — unauthorized acquisition that materially compromises security/confidentiality/integrity of the data **and** causes or is reasonably believed to cause loss or injury to a WY resident.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** **No fixed number of days.** Standard is "the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," consistent with law-enforcement needs and measures to determine breach scope/restore system integrity.
- **AG / consumer reporting agency notice:** No explicit statutory requirement to notify the Wyoming AG or nationwide consumer reporting agencies as a matter of course. § 40-12-502(e)(i) instead requires the notice **to affected individuals** include toll-free contact numbers/addresses for the major credit reporting agencies so individuals can self-report.
- **Payment card + security code covered as "personal identifying information"?** **Not confirmed as explicitly named** in the statutory list reviewed; the statute enumerates a broader set of ~12 categories (SSN, financial account numbers, medical/health insurance information, biometric data used for authentication, among others). Verify current full text of § 40-12-501(a) for the exact enumerated list before relying on card-specific coverage.
- **Encryption/redaction safe harbor:** **No general encryption safe harbor** found in the statute. Safe harbors exist only for: (1) financial institutions complying with GLBA, and (2) HIPAA-covered entities complying with federal health-privacy rules.
- **Penalties:** Not specified as fixed amounts in statute; § 40-12-502(f) authorizes the **Wyoming Attorney General** to bring an action for a violation and recover damages. Each failure to notify an affected individual is treated as a separate violation.
- **Private right of action:** Statute is **silent** on a private right of action; enforcement authority is granted only to the AG. Treat as **no** absent contrary authority — confirm with counsel.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Price-increase notice:** **Not confirmed** — no general Wyoming statute found requiring advance notice before a price increase.
- **Automatic renewal / recurring-charge / subscription-renewal notice:** **Wyoming has no comprehensive automatic-renewal (negative option) statute.** Multiple secondary sources classify WY among states with no or only narrow auto-renewal law. Wyoming does regulate **service contracts** (extended warranties, Title 31 Ch. 14 and Title 26 Ch. 49, administered by the WY Dept. of Insurance for service contract *providers*), which includes a cancellation-notice rule (below), but this is a narrower vehicle-service-contract-style regime, not a general consumer subscription/auto-renewal law.
- **Service contract cancellation notice:** Wyo. Stat. § 31-14-119 area — service contracts must state cancellation terms/conditions, and a provider canceling a contract must mail written notice to the holder's last known address **at least 10 days** prior to cancellation.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** **Not confirmed** — no standalone statute found requiring a cancellation-confirmation notice for general consumer contracts.

## 3. EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Federal Reg E

- **Not confirmed** — no Wyoming-specific statute found imposing preauthorized-transfer notice or change-in-terms notice requirements for recurring consumer debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005). Federal Reg E governs: written/authenticated authorization for preauthorized debits, and a consumer's right to stop payment by notifying the institution at least 3 business days before the scheduled transfer (institution may require written confirmation of an oral stop-payment order within 14 days).
