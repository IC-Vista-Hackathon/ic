# Alabama — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Alabama Data Breach Notification Act of 2018, Ala. Code §§ 8-38-1 to 8-38-12 (Acts 2018-396).
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized acquisition of "sensitive personally identifying information" (SPII) that is reasonably likely to cause substantial harm to affected individuals. Covered entity must conduct a good-faith, reasonable, and prompt investigation.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** As expeditiously as possible and without unreasonable delay; **no later than 45 days** after determination that a breach has occurred (or 45 days after receipt of notice from a third-party agent that a breach occurred). §8-38-5.
- **AG notice:** Required if breach affects **more than 1,000** Alabama residents; same 45-day deadline. Must include breach summary, approximate number of affected residents, remediation/services offered, and contact info. §8-38-6.
- **Consumer reporting agency notice:** Statute does not impose a separate CRA-notification requirement (unlike many other states).
- **Payment card + security code as PII:** **Yes.** SPII includes a financial account number (bank, credit, or debit card) "in combination with any security code, access code, password, expiration date, or PIN" needed to access the account. §8-38-2.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. Data that is truncated, encrypted, secured, or otherwise rendered unusable is excluded from SPII — **unless** the covered entity knows or has reason to know the encryption key/credentials were also compromised. §8-38-2.
- **Penalties:** Violation is an unlawful trade practice under the Alabama Deceptive Trade Practices Act (not a criminal offense). Civil penalty up to **$5,000 per day** of noncompliance, capped at **$500,000 per breach**. AG has exclusive enforcement authority, including recovery of actual damages on behalf of named individuals plus fees/costs. §8-38-9 / §8-19-11.
- **Private right of action:** **No.** Statute explicitly does not create one (preserves other common-law/statutory rights).
- **Substitute notice threshold:** Available if direct-notice cost would exceed $500,000, or the affected class exceeds 100,000 residents, or the entity lacks sufficient contact information (standard "whichever" test — confirm exact numeric thresholds against §8-38-5 text before relying).

## 2. Consumer Payment/Billing Notices

- **Auto-renewal / recurring charge / subscription-renewal notice law:** **None currently in force.** Alabama has no general automatic-renewal statute. A bill (HB405) to require advance notice before auto-renewal for terms over one month was proposed but has not been enacted as of this writing. Not confirmed — re-check current legislative session before relying.
- **Price-increase notice requirement:** Not confirmed — no general Alabama statute located requiring advance notice of a price increase for recurring consumer services.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no general statutory requirement located.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond Reg E)

- No Alabama consumer-facing statute was found imposing preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms notice obligations beyond federal Regulation E (12 CFR Part 1005). Not confirmed — verify with counsel, particularly for any state-specific UCC Article 4A wire-transfer provisions if relevant to commercial (non-consumer) transfers.
