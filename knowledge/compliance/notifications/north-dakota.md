# North Dakota — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify current text and any pending amendments with counsel before relying on this for a specific determination.

## 1. Data Breach Notification

**Statute:** N.D. Cent. Code Ch. 51-30 (Notice of Security Breach for Personal Information), §§ 51-30-01 to 51-30-07

- **Trigger (§ 51-30-01, -02):** Any person/entity conducting business in ND that owns or licenses computerized data including "personal information" must notify affected ND residents after a "breach of the security system" — unauthorized acquisition of computerized data where access to unencrypted personal information occurred (or encryption key was also acquired). Good-faith acquisition by an employee/agent isn't a breach if the data isn't further disclosed.
- **"Personal information" definition:** First name/initial + last name combined with: SSN; driver's license/state ID number; **financial account or credit/debit card number in combination with a required security code, access code, or PIN**; date of birth; mother's maiden name; medical/health insurance information; employer ID number with access code; or electronic signature. **So yes — payment card number + security code is explicitly covered.** Excludes publicly available government-record data.
- **Consumer notice deadline:** No fixed day count — "most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay" (§ 51-30-02).
- **State AG notice:** Required when breach affects **250 or more individuals**, via mail or email (§ 51-30-02).
- **Third-party/processor duty (§ 51-30-03):** An entity that maintains (but doesn't own) the data must notify the owner/licensee "immediately following discovery."
- **Law-enforcement delay (§ 51-30-04):** Notice may be delayed if law enforcement determines it would impede a criminal investigation.
- **Substitute notice (§ 51-30-05):** Available if cost would exceed $250,000 or the affected class exceeds 500,000 (email, conspicuous website posting, and statewide media notice).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — breach defined only where access to *unsecured* (unencrypted) personal information occurred.
- **Alternate compliance (§ 51-30-06):** Entities with their own notification policy consistent with the chapter's timing requirements are deemed compliant; financial institutions/HIPAA-covered entities meeting applicable federal standards are also deemed compliant.
- **Penalties/enforcement (§ 51-30-07):** AG enforces using powers/remedies under Ch. 51-15 (ND's general consumer-fraud/deceptive-practices chapter); a per-violation penalty up to **$5,000** has been reported in secondary sources — **not independently confirmed against statutory text; verify exact figure with counsel.**
- **Private right of action:** None — AG-only enforcement; Ch. 51-15 remedies apply in addition to (not in place of) any other available causes of action.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statute:** N.D. Cent. Code Ch. 51-37 (Customer Contract Clauses and Automatic Renewals), esp. § 51-37-02

- Applies to consumer contracts for merchandise/services with an automatic-renewal clause.
- **Renewal period cap:** Auto-renewal period may not exceed **12 months**.
- **Notice window (renewals >6 months):** Seller must give clear and conspicuous **written** notice of the right to cancel and how to cancel, delivered **at least 30 days and not more than 60 days** before the renewal date (or expiration of the cancellation window).
- **Non-compliance consequence:** If a seller sends merchandise/provides a service under an automatic renewal without complying (or after the buyer affirmatively canceled), the merchandise/service is deemed an **unconditional gift** to the buyer.
- **Price-increase notice (outside auto-renewal):** Not confirmed — no general ND statute requiring advance notice of a price increase for non-auto-renewing recurring charges was found.
- **Cancellation-confirmation notice:** Not confirmed — no explicit requirement found in the text reviewed.
- Effective for contracts entered into after **July 31, 2019** (signed by Gov. Burgum April 9, 2019).
