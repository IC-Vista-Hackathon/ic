# New Jersey — Notification Laws (Internal Compliance Reference)

> **Not legal advice.** Internal reference only — verify all obligations with counsel before acting. Items not independently confirmed against primary sources are flagged below.

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

**Statute:** Identity Theft Prevention Act, N.J. Stat. §§ 56:8-161 (definitions) and 56:8-163 (disclosure of breach).

- **Trigger:** "Breach of security" = unauthorized access to electronic files/media/data containing personal information that compromises its security, confidentiality, or integrity, when the information was not secured by encryption (or other method rendering it unreadable/unusable). Good-faith employee access for legitimate business purposes, without misuse or further disclosure, is not a breach.
- **Deadline to notify affected consumers:** "In the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay," consistent with legitimate law-enforcement needs and measures to determine scope/restore integrity — **no fixed numeric day deadline** in the statute (unlike NY/NM's fixed-day standards).
- **Law enforcement notification:** Mandatory and must occur **in advance of** consumer disclosure — business/public entity must report the breach to the NJ Division of State Police (Dept. of Law & Public Safety). No numeric threshold; applies to any breach requiring consumer notice.
- **AG notification / CRA threshold:** Not confirmed as a distinct numeric-threshold requirement in the sources reviewed for § 56:8-163 itself (NJ's law is comparatively light on a separate AG-notice trigger vs. NY/NM) — **verify with counsel** for current AG-notice practice.
- **Email account exception:** If breach involves an email account the business itself furnishes, notice cannot be sent to that compromised email address; must use another method in the statute (or clear/conspicuous online notice when the customer is known to access the account from a given IP/location).
- **"Personal information" — payment card coverage:** Yes, explicitly covered. Definition (§ 56:8-161) includes "account number or credit or debit card number, in combination with any required security code, access code, or password" — this expressly reaches a card number + CVV/security code pairing.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes. If the compromised data was secured by encryption (or another method rendering it unreadable/unusable) and the encryption key/security credential was not also compromised, notification is not required.
- **Penalties:** Civil penalty — not more than **$10,000 for a first offense**, not more than **$20,000 for a second and each subsequent offense** — in addition to any other penalty provided by law.
- **Private right of action:** **Yes.** NJ's breach law sits within the Consumer Fraud Act (N.J. Stat. § 56:8-1 et seq.), which provides a private right of action with **treble damages** — unlike most states where only the AG enforces the breach statute. This is a materially higher-risk posture than NY/NH/NM for civil exposure.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

**Statute:** N.J. Stat. § 56:12-95 et seq. (automatic-renewal / subscription law, enacted 2024).

- **Scope:** Service contracts with an initial term of **12 months or longer** that automatically renew for a period of **more than one month**, unless canceled.
- **Renewal notice window:** Written or electronic notice of the automatic-renewal provision required **not less than 30 days nor more than 60 days** before the cancellation deadline. Must clearly/conspicuously disclose that the contract will auto-renew unless canceled and describe cancellation methods (minimum: an online method + mailing address, or a phone number).
- **Cancellation mechanics:** Business must provide a toll-free number, email, postal address, or other cost-effective cancellation mechanism; consumers who enrolled online must be able to cancel entirely online.
- **Cancellation confirmation:** All cancellation requests must be **acknowledged within 5 business days** of receipt and **honored within 10 business days** of receipt.
- **Non-compliance remedy:** If a seller fails to comply, the auto-renewal provision is **void and unenforceable**, and the seller must refund the unearned portion of the renewed service contract.
- No separate price-increase-specific advance-notice rule (distinct from the renewal notice above) was confirmed in the sources reviewed — **verify with counsel** whether NJ has adopted anything analogous to NY's 2025 material-change notice.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (Beyond Federal Reg E)

- N.J. Stat. § 12A:4A-108 (UCC Article 4A funds transfers) provides that where NJ's funds-transfer chapter conflicts with the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act, **federal EFTA governs** — i.e., NJ statutorily defers to federal Reg E rather than layering additional preauthorized-transfer notice requirements.
- No NJ-specific notice-of-preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms requirement beyond Reg E was identified. **Not confirmed exhaustively — verify with counsel.**
