# West Virginia — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially anything marked "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification

- **Statute:** W. Va. Code §§ 46A-2A-101 to 46A-2A-105 (Breach of Security of Consumer Information).
  - § 46A-2A-101: definitions.
  - § 46A-2A-102: notification obligations (consumer, CRA notice, substitute notice).
- **Trigger:** Unauthorized access **and acquisition** of unencrypted and unredacted computerized "personal information" that causes the entity to reasonably believe it has caused or will cause identity theft or fraud to a WV resident. Good-faith acquisition by an employee/agent for the entity's own purposes, not used for unauthorized disclosure, is excluded from the definition of "breach."
- **Consumer notice deadline:** "Without unreasonable delay" following discovery/notification — **no fixed day count** in the statute (delay permitted to determine scope/restore system integrity, or for law-enforcement request).
- **AG / CRA notice:** If notice is required to **more than 1,000 persons**, the entity must also notify, **without unreasonable delay**, all nationwide consumer reporting agencies. No fixed day count found for this trigger either. Enforcement authority (including any AG pre-notice requirement) sits with the WV Attorney General under § 46A-2A-104(b).
- **Substitute notice available if:** cost of notice would exceed **$50,000**, or the affected class exceeds **100,000** persons, or the entity lacks sufficient contact information.
- **Payment card number + security code covered as "personal information":** **Yes, explicitly** — "financial account number, or credit card, or debit card number in combination with any required security code, access code, or password," when linked to a name + SSN/ID-type identifier.
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Trigger is limited to "unencrypted and unredacted" data, functioning as an implicit safe harbor for properly encrypted/redacted data — no separate explicit "safe harbor" clause beyond that scoping was identified.
- **Penalties:** Not specified within §§ 46A-2A-101/-102 as reviewed; § 46A-2A-104(b) vests **exclusive enforcement authority in the WV Attorney General** (implying a civil-penalty/injunctive framework typical of WV Consumer Credit and Protection Act enforcement, but the specific penalty amount was **not confirmed** in the sections retrieved — verify §§ 46A-2A-103–105 directly with counsel).
- **Private right of action:** **No** — § 46A-2A-104(b) explicitly vests exclusive enforcement in the WV AG; consumers cannot sue privately under Article 2A for a notification failure.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **Automatic renewal / continuous service law:** W. Va. Code §§ 46A-6O-1 to 46A-6O-6 (or successor numbering — cite by session-law title "relating to automatic purchase renewal offers and continuous service offers"; confirm final codified article number with counsel, as multiple bill versions (SB 253/2019, SB 33/2022, SB 134/2023, HB 2851/2025) have amended/re-proposed this framework and the currently operative citation should be pulled fresh from the WV Code site before relying on it).
  - Legislative intent: end the practice of ongoing charges to a consumer's card/third-party payment account **without explicit consent** for recurring shipments/services.
  - "Automatic renewal" = a paid subscription/purchasing agreement automatically renewed at the end of a definite term, for a specified period of **more than one month**.
  - Requires an **acknowledgment** (capable of being retained by the consumer) disclosing the automatic-renewal/continuous-service terms, the cancellation policy, and how to cancel.
  - For **free gifts/trials**, the acknowledgment must also disclose how to cancel **before the consumer is charged**.
  - Must provide a cancellation mechanism: toll-free number, email, postal address (if directly billing), or another described mechanism; online sign-ups must be cancellable online.
  - **No charge without affirmative consent** — cannot bill for auto-renewal/continuous service without first obtaining the consumer's affirmative consent.
  - **Private right of action:** consumer alleging harm may bring a civil action for injunctive relief or damages, with a statutory penalty of **$1,000 per violation**.
- **Price-increase-specific notice / cancellation-confirmation notice beyond the acknowledgment above:** Not confirmed as a separate distinct requirement — the acknowledgment/consent regime above is the operative mechanism; verify current codified section with counsel given the multiple legislative revisions.

## 3. State EFT/ACH Notice Requirements Beyond Reg E

- WV's UCC funds-transfer article (Article 46-4A) does not apply to transfers governed by the federal Electronic Fund Transfer Act — consumer preauthorized/recurring EFTs fall to federal Reg E, not a distinct WV consumer regime.
- No West Virginia-specific statute imposing notice-of-preauthorized-transfer or change-in-terms obligations beyond Reg E (12 CFR 1005.10) was identified in this review.
- **Not confirmed — verify with counsel** whether WV Consumer Credit and Protection Act (Ch. 46A) provisions outside Article 2A (breach) or 6O (auto-renewal) impose any additional EFT-specific consumer notice duties; a full Chapter 46A sweep was not completed.
