# Texas — Notification Law Reference

**Disclaimer:** Internal compliance reference only. Not legal advice. Verify with counsel before relying on any item below, especially those flagged "Not confirmed."

## 1. Data Breach Notification Law

- **Statute:** Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Chapter 521 (Identity Theft Enforcement and Protection Act), notice provision at § 521.053; penalties at § 521.151.
- **Trigger:** "Breach of system security" = unauthorized acquisition of computerized data that compromises the security, confidentiality, or integrity of sensitive personal information (including encrypted data if the intruder also has the decryption key), maintained by a person conducting business in Texas. Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 521.053(a)-(b).
- **Consumer notice deadline:** Without unreasonable delay, and **not later than the 60th day** after the date the person determines the breach occurred. § 521.053(b).
- **State AG notification threshold/deadline:** Notice required for **any breach requiring notification to Texas residents**; must be reported to the Texas AG **as soon as practicable and not later than 30 days** after determining the breach occurred (shortened from 60 days by SB 768, effective Sept. 1, 2023), via the AG's electronic breach-report form. § 521.053(i)-(j) (subsection numbering per 2023 amendment — confirm current cite with counsel).
- **Payment card + security code covered as "sensitive personal information":** **Yes.** Defined as first name/initial + last name combined with, among other elements, an account number or credit/debit card number **in combination with any required security code, access code, or password permitting access to the individual's financial account** — when the name and the data element are not encrypted. § 521.002(a)(2).
- **Encryption safe harbor:** Yes — encrypted sensitive personal information falls outside the trigger, **unless the encryption key was also acquired** in the breach. § 521.053(a)-(b).
- **Penalties:** Civil penalty of **$2,000–$50,000 per violation**, payable to the state; additional penalty of **up to $100 per individual per day** for failure to reasonably comply with the notification timing in § 521.053(b), capped at **$250,000 per breach**. § 521.151(a)-(b).
- **Private right of action:** No direct PRA under Chapter 521 itself, but a violation may constitute a deceptive trade practice actionable under the Texas DTPA (Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 17) — **indirect** route only; confirm viability with counsel given DTPA's own standing/notice requirements.

## 2. Consumer-Facing Payment/Billing Notices

- **New subscription-service law:** **SB 838** (89th Legislature, 2025), amending Tex. Bus. & Com. Code (exact new chapter number **not confirmed** from sources reviewed — verify before citing). Applies to contracts entered/renewed **on or after Sept. 1, 2025**.
  - Requires clear disclosure of the automatic-renewal nature of the contract and a clear cancellation procedure at contract formation.
  - Requires **written notice 15–90 days before renewal** for contracts with a **12-month or longer term** that will auto-renew.
  - Requires multiple cancellation methods, including a method matching how the consumer entered the contract (e.g., online cancellation for an online sign-up) — relevant to cancellation-confirmation practice.
  - Enforcement: Texas AG only; civil penalty up to **$2,000 per violation per month**; statute **explicitly bars private lawsuits**.
- **Price-increase notice (general, outside auto-renewal context):** **Not confirmed** — no general Texas statute located requiring advance notice of an ordinary (non-renewal) price increase.
- **Door-to-door/home-solicitation cancellation:** Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Chapter 601 provides a 3-day cancellation right and related receipt/notice requirements for certain consumer transactions (home solicitation sales), but this is **not** an autopay/recurring-charge notice regime — distinct from the SB 838 subscription law. § 601.001 et seq.

## 3. State-Specific EFT/ACH Notice Requirements (beyond federal Reg E)

- **Not confirmed.** No Texas-specific statute identified imposing notice requirements for preauthorized EFT/recurring ACH debits beyond federal Reg E (12 CFR Part 1005)/EFTA. Texas UCC Article 4A (funds transfers, Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 4A) governs wholesale wire transfers, not consumer preauthorized debits, and expressly yields to the EFTA where both could apply.
